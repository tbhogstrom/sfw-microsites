## Deck Post Replacement
Replacing deck posts is a critical repair for ensuring the stability and safety of your deck, especially in Portland's wet climate. Over time, moisture can penetrate wooden deck posts, leading to rot and structural failure. Common signs that your deck posts may need replacement include visible rot, insect damage, or a noticeable shift in deck stability. The process starts by carefully assessing each post's condition. If a post is compromised, it should be replaced with a new pressure-treated lumber post, which provides better resistance to moisture<sup>1</sup>. During installation, it's essential to use metal post bases to prevent contact with the ground, thereby minimizing moisture exposure and prolonging the post's lifespan<sup>2</sup>. Additionally, ensure the new post is secured properly to the deck structure to maintain stability. By replacing damaged posts promptly, you can prevent further damage to your deck and maintain its safety and usability. Ready to ensure your deck's safety? Contact us for a professional assessment and replace those worn-out posts today.

## Footing Repairs & Replacement
Footing repairs and replacements are essential for maintaining the safety and stability of your deck, especially in the Pacific Northwest where moisture and soil conditions can be challenging. Proper footing ensures that your deck remains level and secure, preventing issues like sagging or tilting. When assessing the need for footing repairs, it's crucial to consider the soil's load-bearing capacity and ensure that footings are installed on undisturbed soil or engineered fill<sup>3</sup>. In Portland's climate, where moderate to severe weathering occurs, using concrete with the appropriate compressive strength is vital for longevity<sup>4</sup>. If you notice signs of footing failure, such as unusual deck movement or visible cracks around the footings, it's time to take action. A professional evaluation and repair can prevent further damage and ensure your deck remains safe for use. Ensure your deck's foundation is rock-solid by scheduling a footing inspection today.

## Deck Concrete Pier Repair
Repairing concrete piers is crucial for maintaining the structural integrity of your deck, especially in the Pacific Northwest where rain and moisture are constant threats. Concrete piers may deteriorate over time due to water infiltration, which can lead to cracks and instability. A critical step in repairing these piers involves assessing the extent of the damage and ensuring the foundation below the frost line is secure<sup>5</sup>. This often requires removing any compromised concrete and replacing it with a fresh mix that includes air-entraining admixtures. These admixtures help prevent cracking by allowing the concrete to expand and contract with temperature changes<sup>6</sup>. Properly repairing your deck's concrete piers not only enhances the safety and stability of your deck but also extends its lifespan. If you notice any signs of wear or damage to your deck's piers, it's important to address these issues promptly. Contact us to schedule a professional inspection and secure your deck's foundation today.

## Deck Shoring & Safety
Deck shoring and safety are critical components when ensuring the longevity and structural integrity of your deck, especially in the damp climate of Portland, Oregon. Shoring involves reinforcing the deck's existing structure to prevent collapse or sagging, which is particularly important in older decks that have been exposed to moisture and rot over time. Utilizing metal framing aids, such as post bases and caps, can enhance the strength of the connections between deck components and eliminate the need for toenailing, which can split the wood and compromise the structure<sup>7</sup>. Additionally, using pressure-treated wood for posts and beams provides an extra layer of protection against the pervasive Pacific Northwest moisture, ensuring that the deck remains safe and sturdy for years to come<sup>8</sup>. Regular inspection and timely repairs of any weakened areas can prevent more extensive damage and maintain the safety of your outdoor space. If you suspect your deck needs shoring, contact us to schedule an inspection and keep your deck safe and secure.

## Deck Leveling & Stabilization
When it comes to deck leveling and stabilization in the Pacific Northwest, addressing the unique challenges posed by the region's damp climate is essential. The constant exposure to moisture can lead to soil erosion, which affects the stability of your deck's foundation. Ensuring that your deck remains level and stable involves examining and potentially reinforcing the support structures like beams and posts. One effective method is using pressure-treated posts, which resist decay and are placed on concrete footings below the frost line to prevent shifting<sup>9</sup>. Additionally, incorporating metal framing aids such as hurricane ties and rigid ties can significantly enhance the deck's structural integrity<sup>10</sup>. These components help secure the joists and beams, reducing the risk of movement over time. If your deck is showing signs of unevenness or instability, it's crucial to address these issues promptly to prevent further damage. Schedule an inspection with our team to evaluate your deck's condition and discuss the best stabilization options for your home.

## Deck Support Post Repair
Deck support post repair is crucial for maintaining the safety and stability of your deck, especially in the damp climate of Portland, Oregon. Over time, moisture can seep into wooden support posts, leading to rot and weakening their structural integrity<sup>11</sup>. This deterioration can be exacerbated by improper installation that lacks protective measures, such as pressure-treated lumber or metal post bases that prevent direct contact with the ground<sup>12</sup>. When repairing deck support posts, it's essential to replace any compromised wood with pressure-treated alternatives, which are more resistant to moisture damage. Additionally, installing post bases can help elevate the posts above the ground, minimizing exposure to moisture and prolonging their lifespan. If your deck's support posts show signs of damage, such as visible rot or instability, it is important to address the issue promptly to prevent further deterioration and potential safety hazards. Schedule an inspection to assess the condition of your deck support posts and determine the best course of action for repairs.
