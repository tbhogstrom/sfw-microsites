## Deck Ledger Board Replacement
Replacing a deck ledger board is a critical task that ensures the structural integrity of your deck, especially in Seattle's damp climate. The ledger board serves as the primary anchor point between your deck and home, and any sign of rot or weakness can compromise the entire structure. It's essential to use a pressure-treated 2x8 ledger board, as outlined in design standards, to prevent moisture damage and ensure longevity<sup>1</sup>. Correct installation involves fastening the ledger board securely to the house's structure using lag screws or bolts, a method that provides the necessary support against the weight and movement of the deck<sup>2</sup>. Additionally, incorporating a proper flashing system above the ledger board is vital to divert water away and prevent wood decay. This step is particularly important in the Pacific Northwest, where rain is frequent and can penetrate unprotected wood joints. If you notice any issues with your deck ledger board, prompt replacement is vital to maintain safety and prevent further damage. Contact us today to evaluate and replace your deck ledger board, ensuring your deck remains safe and sturdy for years to come.

## Deck Ledger Repairs & Flashing
Deck ledger repairs and flashing are critical components in maintaining the integrity of your deck, especially in Seattle's wet climate. The deck ledger, which connects the deck to your home, must be securely fastened and properly flashed to prevent water infiltration and rot. Over time, moisture can deteriorate the ledger board, leading to structural weakness<sup>3</sup>. To ensure a robust connection, using appropriate fasteners and flashing methods is essential. Metal flashing should be installed above the ledger board and sealed with a compatible elastomeric sealant to create a barrier against water<sup>4</sup>.

If your deck ledger is showing signs of damage, such as wood rot or loose connections, it’s crucial to address these issues promptly. Repairing or replacing the ledger board involves removing any compromised sections and reinforcing the connection with pressure-treated wood and corrosion-resistant fasteners. Proper flashing should be reinstated to safeguard against future water damage.

Protect your home and deck from Seattle's relentless rain by ensuring your deck ledger and flashing are up to standard. If you suspect issues with your deck ledger, schedule an inspection with our team today to prevent further damage.

## Deck Flashing Installation or Repair
Proper deck flashing installation or repair is crucial to prevent water damage and extend the lifespan of your deck. In Seattle's damp climate, water intrusion is a common problem, especially around deck ledger boards. Flashing acts as a barrier that directs water away from vulnerable areas of your deck, such as where the deck attaches to your home. Correctly sized and positioned step flashing should be installed to ensure water is directed away from the structure<sup>5</sup>. Improperly installed flashing can lead to wood rot, mold, and structural issues. The flashing should extend under siding and over the ledger board, with the goal of directing water towards the exterior, thus protecting your home from water damage<sup>6</sup>. If you suspect your deck's flashing is compromised, addressing it promptly can save you from extensive repairs down the line. For expert deck flashing installation or repair in Seattle, assess your deck's current condition and contact a local specialist to ensure your deck remains safe and dry.

## Deck Waterproofing Repairs
Consistent exposure to Seattle's persistent rain can compromise your deck's waterproofing, leading to costly repairs if not addressed promptly. Waterproofing membranes, such as self-adhering waterproof shingle underlayment (WSU), provide an effective barrier against moisture intrusion. These membranes self-seal around nails, maintaining a waterproof seal even at fastener points<sup>7</sup>. Liquid membranes, either solvent or water-based, are another option often applied to a uniform thickness to ensure complete coverage. They require professional installation to avoid trapping water beneath the surface, which can lead to blisters and eventual failure<sup>8</sup>. Proper flashing around deck perimeters and intersections is crucial to direct water away and prevent leaks. If your deck's waterproofing shows signs of deterioration, such as bubbling or peeling, it's time to take action. Contact us to assess and repair your deck's waterproofing, ensuring it withstands Seattle's wet climate while protecting your investment.

## Deck Drainage Corrections
When it comes to maintaining the integrity of your deck in Seattle's rainy climate, proper drainage is key. Without an efficient drainage system, water can collect on your deck, leading to wood rot and structural damage over time. A well-designed drainage system will ensure water is directed away from your deck, protecting it from the Pacific Northwest's notorious moisture. To achieve this, consider installing a perforated pipe drainage system, which can effectively redirect water by using a sloped design to guide it away from the deck<sup>9</sup>. Additionally, using pressure-treated wood for posts and framing can prevent water damage, as this material is more resistant to moisture-related deterioration<sup>10</sup>. If you notice pooling water or damp spots on your deck, it's time to address your drainage issues promptly. Reach out to our team for expert solutions tailored to your home's needs.
