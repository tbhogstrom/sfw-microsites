## 7. FAQ Section
**1. What causes wood trim to rot?**
Rotting is typically caused by prolonged exposure to moisture and lack of proper maintenance. 

**2. How can I tell if my trim is rotting?**
Look for signs such as peeling paint, soft or crumbly wood, and water stains ([Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 207](#)).

**3. Can you repair just a section of trim?**
Yes, we can often repair specific sections instead of replacing the entire trim ([Source: Christina Glennon - Siding, Roofing, and Trim, Page 196](#)).

**4. How long does the repair process take?**
The duration varies based on the extent of the damage, but most repairs can be completed in a day.

**5. Will the repairs be visible?**
We strive for a seamless finish, often using matching materials and techniques to blend repairs with existing trim ([Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 173](#)).

**6. What materials do you recommend for trim replacement?**
We recommend durable options such as PVC, which is rot-resistant and low-maintenance ([Source: Christina Glennon - Siding, Roofing, and Trim, Page 195](#)).

**7. Is your work guaranteed?**
Yes, we stand behind our work and offer guarantees on our repairs to ensure your satisfaction.

**8. How do I maintain my trim after repair?**
Regular inspections and maintenance, including repainting and sealing, will help prolong the life of your trim.
