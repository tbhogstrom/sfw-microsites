## Deck Board Removal & Refastening
Removing and refastening deck boards is an essential maintenance task for Seattle homeowners, especially given the Pacific Northwest's wet climate that can lead to wood expansion and contraction. To start, carefully remove the existing deck boards using a pry bar or a drill to remove screws or nails. Ensure minimal damage by working slowly and systematically, especially near the ends of the boards where splitting is more likely. Once the boards are removed, inspect the underlying joists for any signs of rot or damage before proceeding with refastening. When reinstalling the boards, consider using corrosion-resistant screws, which perform better in moist environments compared to nails<sup>1</sup>. This method provides a stronger hold and can prevent the boards from loosening over time. Additionally, leave a small gap between the boards to allow for natural expansion and contraction<sup>2</sup>. This helps maintain the integrity of your deck surface and ensures proper drainage. If you're unsure about the condition of your deck structure, reach out to SFW Construction for a professional evaluation and expert assistance in keeping your deck safe and sturdy.

## Rotten Deck Board Replacement
Replacing rotten deck boards is crucial to maintaining the structural integrity and appearance of your deck, especially in the moist climate of Seattle. The first step involves identifying the extent of the rot, which typically starts at the ends of the boards where moisture accumulates. Using a circular saw, carefully cut out the damaged section, ensuring the cut is made to expose sound wood<sup>3</sup>. Once removed, it's important to select a replacement board that matches the original in species and grain to maintain a consistent look and ensure durability<sup>4</sup>. Before installation, treat the cut ends of the new board with a wood preservative to protect against future rot. Secure the new board using appropriate fasteners, ensuring a snug fit to prevent moisture penetration. Regular maintenance and timely replacements help prolong the life of your deck, making it a safe and enjoyable space. If your deck boards are showing signs of rot, contact us to discuss the best replacement options for your home.

## Deck Surface Repair & Refinishing
Repairing and refinishing your deck surface in Seattle is essential for maintaining its beauty and functionality, especially given the region's persistent rain and moisture. Over time, deck surfaces can become worn, leading to splintering wood and faded finishes. To restore the deck, we start by addressing any damaged areas. This may involve replacing broken or rotted boards, ensuring that all surface components are structurally sound<sup>5</sup>. Next, we prepare the surface by thoroughly sanding it to remove old finishes and create a smooth base, which is crucial for the new stain or sealant to adhere properly<sup>6</sup>.

Once the surface is prepped, we apply a high-quality stain or sealant designed for the Pacific Northwest's wet climate. This step not only enhances the wood's appearance but also offers protection against moisture and UV damage, prolonging the life of your deck. Regular maintenance, including refinishing every few years, is recommended to keep your deck looking its best and functioning safely. To get started on your deck surface repair and refinishing, reach out to schedule an inspection and discuss your options.

## Touch-Up Sanding & Staining
Revitalize your deck with our touch-up sanding and staining services, perfect for Seattle’s damp climate. Over time, moisture and heavy rainfall can cause wear and tear on your deck's surface, leading to discoloration and uneven texture. Touch-up sanding is an effective way to smooth out these imperfections, removing old stains and leveling the wood surface to prepare it for a fresh coat of stain. Using open-coat sandpaper for the initial sanding pass ensures efficient removal of old finishes without damaging the wood<sup>7</sup>. Once the surface is prepped, choosing the right stain is crucial. Water-based polyurethanes are ideal for maintaining light-colored wood tones, while oil-based options bring a rich, amber hue to darker woods<sup>8</sup>. Both options can offer enhanced protection against Seattle's wet weather, extending the life of your deck. Ready to restore your deck's natural beauty? ## Deck Fascia Board Repair
Deck fascia boards play a crucial role in maintaining the structural integrity and aesthetic appeal of your deck. In Seattle's damp climate, these boards are particularly prone to rot and damage, which can compromise the overall health of your deck. Repairing or replacing damaged fascia boards promptly is essential to prevent further decay and maintain the deck's appearance. When addressing fascia board repair, it is important to cut back to undamaged wood, ideally at a 45° angle known as a scarf joint, to ensure a strong bond with new wood sections<sup>9</sup>. Using high-strength epoxy rather than traditional carpenter's glue can create a more durable joint capable of withstanding Seattle's wet conditions<sup>10</sup>. If your deck fascia is showing signs of wear, it might be time to inspect and repair any damage. Start by evaluating the extent of the decay and deciding whether a simple repair or complete replacement is required. For a lasting solution, contact SFW Construction to handle your fascia board repair with expertise tailored for the Pacific Northwest.

## Deck Rim Joist Repair
The rim joist is a crucial component of your deck's structural integrity, acting as a key support element that connects the deck to your home<sup>11</sup>. In the damp climate of Seattle, moisture can infiltrate and lead to wood rot, compromising the strength of the rim joist over time<sup>12</sup>. Timely repair of a damaged rim joist is essential to prevent further structural issues and ensure the safety and longevity of your deck. Our repair process involves assessing the extent of the damage, removing any compromised material, and replacing it with pressure-treated wood to resist future moisture issues. We ensure that the connections, such as lag bolts and joist hangers, are secure and properly installed to maintain the deck’s stability<sup>11</sup>. Don’t let a weakened rim joist jeopardize your deck’s safety—schedule an inspection today to assess your repair needs.
