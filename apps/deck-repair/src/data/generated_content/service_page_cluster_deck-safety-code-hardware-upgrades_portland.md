## Deck Safety Upgrades to Current Code
Ensuring your deck meets current safety codes is crucial for both legal compliance and peace of mind. In Portland's rainy climate, moisture can accelerate wear and decay, making it vital to adhere to stringent safety standards. For decks more than 30 inches above the ground, guardrails must be a minimum of 36 inches high, with openings no larger than 4 inches to prevent accidents<sup>1</sup>. This is not just about following regulations; it's about safeguarding your family and guests. Additionally, the framing should be robust enough to withstand the Pacific Northwest's weather conditions. This involves using pressure-treated wood for structural components to resist rot and using metal framing aids like hurricane ties to secure the deck structure<sup>2</sup>. Upgrading to these materials and techniques not only enhances safety but also prolongs the life of your deck. For homeowners in Portland, ensuring your deck aligns with these codes and standards is a proactive step toward safety and longevity. If you're concerned about your deck's compliance, schedule an inspection with us today to identify necessary upgrades.

## Deck Hardware Replacement
Replacing deck hardware is crucial for maintaining the safety and longevity of your deck, especially in the damp Pacific Northwest climate. Over time, metal components such as screws, bolts, and connectors can corrode, weakening the structural integrity of your deck. Stainless steel screws are highly recommended due to their superior corrosion resistance, making them particularly suitable for the rainy weather in Portland<sup>3</sup>. When replacing hardware, consider upgrading to modern connectors like Simpson Strong-Tie products, which offer enhanced durability and safety. These connectors, such as hurricane ties and joist hangers, are designed to secure the deck's structural components, providing additional stability against wind and seismic activity<sup>4</sup>. Regular inspection and replacement of deck hardware can prevent accidents and costly repairs in the future. If you notice rust, looseness, or any signs of wear in your deck hardware, it's time to upgrade. Schedule a professional assessment to ensure your deck is safe and up to current standards.

## Deck Connector Upgrades (Simpson Hardware)
Upgrading your deck connectors to Simpson Hardware is a smart decision for enhanced safety and longevity. Known for their strength and reliability, Simpson connectors are metal construction aids that provide superior wood-to-wood connections. Unlike traditional construction methods, these connectors minimize the risk of splitting lumber ends and loosening over time<sup>5</sup>. In Portland's rainy climate, it's crucial to use robust hardware to resist the elements. Simpson Strong-Tie® products, such as post caps, post-to-beam connectors, and hurricane ties, are designed to withstand harsh conditions and prevent structural failure<sup>6</sup>. ## Joist Hanger Repairs or Upgrades
Joist hangers are crucial to the structural integrity of your deck, as they provide essential support for the joists connecting to beams and ledgers. In the Pacific Northwest, where moisture is a constant concern, rust and deterioration can compromise these fittings over time. Replacing or upgrading joist hangers with corrosion-resistant options, such as those made from stainless steel or galvanized metal, ensures long-term durability<sup>7</sup>. Modern joist hangers, like those from Simpson Strong-Tie, offer enhanced load-bearing capabilities and are designed to integrate seamlessly with existing structures, making them an ideal choice for renovations<sup>8</sup>. If your deck feels shaky or you notice visible signs of wear on the hangers, it's time to consider an upgrade. Secure your deck's safety and extend its lifespan by opting for professional joist hanger repairs or upgrades today.

## Deck Structural Reinforcement
When it comes to ensuring the longevity and safety of your deck, structural reinforcement is crucial, especially given the rainy and humid conditions in Portland. Reinforcement typically involves the strategic addition of supports, such as beams, joists, and post bases, to strengthen the deck's framework<sup>9</sup>. Utilizing pressure-treated lumber for posts and beams is essential in our climate to prevent rot and decay<sup>10</sup>. Additionally, metal framing aids like post caps and bases can significantly enhance the structural integrity by providing robust connections that resist splitting and shifting<sup>9</sup>. Reinforcing joists with hurricane ties or similar connectors can further stabilize the deck against the strong winds and heavy rains typical of the Pacific Northwest<sup>10</sup>. To ensure your deck is safe and secure, especially during the stormy months, consider scheduling a structural assessment and reinforcement service tailored to your specific needs.
