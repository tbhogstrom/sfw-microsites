## Deck Board Removal & Refastening
Removing and refastening deck boards is a crucial step in maintaining a safe and appealing outdoor space, especially in the wet climate of Portland. Over time, deck boards can loosen due to the expansion and contraction caused by moisture. To remove a damaged board, begin by drilling holes across it to facilitate prying it out in sections, or use a circular saw set to the depth of the flooring to cut it out<sup>1</sup>. Once removed, ensure the underlying joists are in good condition before installing new boards. When refastening, it's essential to drill and face-nail the new plank slightly shorter than the space to account for expansion, allowing a 1/8-inch caulk joint at the ends<sup>2</sup>. This ensures a secure fit and helps prevent further water damage. By addressing loose boards promptly, you can extend the life of your deck and maintain its structural integrity. Ready to tackle your deck repair? Start by inspecting your deck for loose or damaged boards and make a plan to secure them properly.

## Rotten Deck Board Replacement
Replacing rotten deck boards is crucial to maintaining the safety and aesthetics of your outdoor space in the damp Pacific Northwest climate. The process begins by identifying which boards need replacement, typically indicated by soft spots or visible decay. To replace these boards, carefully remove the damaged sections by cutting them at a 90-degree angle to ensure a clean edge, as this helps create a seamless fit with new boards<sup>3</sup>. It's essential to choose replacement boards that match the original in size, thickness, and preferably the species of the wood, ensuring durability and a consistent appearance<sup>4</sup>.

Once you've selected the new boards, set them into place, ensuring they're securely fastened with galvanized or stainless steel screws to prevent future corrosion. This is especially important in the moist environment of Portland, where untreated fasteners can quickly deteriorate. Seal the new boards with a weather-resistant finish to protect against moisture infiltration and extend the life of your deck. For Pacific Northwest homeowners, regular inspection and timely replacement of rotten boards are key to preserving the integrity of your deck. When you're ready to replace those deteriorating boards, ensure your new timber is sourced and installed to withstand our unique climate.

## Deck Surface Repair & Refinishing
Deck surface repair and refinishing are essential for maintaining the beauty and longevity of your deck, especially in the damp climate of the Pacific Northwest. Over time, exposure to rain and moisture can lead to a faded appearance, surface cracks, and even the growth of mold and moss. To restore your deck to its original condition, it's important to begin by thoroughly cleaning the surface, removing any dirt, debris, and organic growth<sup>5</sup>. After cleaning, sanding the deck will smooth out any rough patches and remove old, flaking finish, preparing the surface for refinishing. Applying a high-quality sealant or stain will not only enhance the deck's appearance but also provide a protective barrier against moisture and UV damage<sup>6</sup>. Regular refinishing can significantly extend the life of your deck, protecting it from the elements and keeping it looking its best. If your deck is showing signs of wear, now is the time to consider a professional surface repair and refinishing to preserve your investment.

## Touch-Up Sanding & Staining
In the Pacific Northwest, where constant rain can wear down your deck, touch-up sanding and staining are essential to maintain its appearance and longevity. Sanding smooths out rough patches and removes old stains, making the deck ready for a fresh coat. Start with a palm sander using 80-grit sandpaper to feather edges and smooth uneven surfaces<sup>7</sup>. This helps new stains adhere well, ensuring a uniform finish. For staining, use a water-based polyurethane if you want to maintain the wood's natural light color, or opt for an oil-based polyurethane for a richer, amber hue<sup>8</sup>. Remember, a well-prepared surface absorbs stain better, protecting your deck from moisture and UV damage. To keep your deck looking its best, schedule regular touch-up sanding and staining sessions before the rainy season hits.

## Deck Fascia Board Repair
Deck fascia boards play a crucial role in protecting your deck's structural integrity and enhancing its aesthetic appeal. In Portland, the combination of regular rainfall and moisture can lead to rot and decay in these boards, especially if they were originally built with untreated wood. To effectively repair a damaged fascia board, it's essential to identify the extent of the rot. If only a small section is affected, a high-strength epoxy may be used to create a durable repair that can last as long as the wood itself<sup>9</sup>. For more extensive damage, it's often necessary to replace the entire length of the board. This ensures that the new fascia integrates seamlessly with the existing structure and prevents further deterioration<sup>10</sup>. Proper installation is key, as it involves securely fastening the new board and ensuring it is well-protected against moisture and the elements. If you're noticing signs of fascia board damage, such as peeling paint, warping, or visible rot, it's time to assess the situation and plan for repair or replacement to maintain your deck's longevity and appearance.

Take the first step in preserving your deck by scheduling a professional fascia board inspection today.

## Deck Rim Joist Repair
In Portland's damp climate, deck rim joists are particularly vulnerable to moisture damage. The rim joist serves as a crucial structural element, running along the perimeter of the deck and supporting the floor joists. When moisture penetrates, it can lead to rot and compromise the integrity of your deck. If you notice signs of damage, such as soft spots or visible decay, it's essential to act quickly. The repair process typically involves removing the damaged section and replacing it with new, pressure-treated wood to ensure longevity and resistance to future moisture intrusion<sup>11</sup>. Proper installation includes using metal connectors and ensuring secure attachments to prevent future issues<sup>12</sup>. To maintain the safety and durability of your deck, regular inspections and timely repairs are key. If you suspect your deck's rim joist is compromised, schedule an inspection with our team to assess and address any issues before they escalate.
