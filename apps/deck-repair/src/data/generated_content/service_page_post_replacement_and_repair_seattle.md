## Service Overview

### What is Post Replacement and Repair?

Post replacement and repair involve the assessment, repair, or complete replacement of deck posts that have deteriorated due to moisture, rot, or structural damage. This service is essential for maintaining the safety, functionality, and aesthetics of your deck.

### Why It's Important for Homeowners in Seattle, Washington

Seattle's climate, characterized by high humidity and frequent rainfall, can lead to accelerated wear and tear on wooden structures, particularly deck posts. Over time, moisture can cause wood to rot, compromising the structural integrity of your deck. Regular inspections and timely repairs can prevent costly damages and ensure your outdoor space remains safe for gatherings and leisure.

### Common Problems This Service Solves

- **Rotting Posts:** Often caused by prolonged exposure to moisture. [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 253]
- **Sinking or Uneven Decks:** Resulting from failing posts or pads. [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 252]
- **Aesthetic Damage:** Deteriorated posts can detract from your deck's overall appearance.

## Our Process

### Step-by-Step Breakdown

1. **Initial Assessment**
   - Our experienced team inspects your deck to identify rotting or damaged posts and assess structural integrity. We look for signs of moisture damage and evaluate the need for repair or replacement.

2. **Planning & Preparation**
   - We develop a comprehensive plan tailored to your deck's specific issues. This includes selecting high-quality, pressure-treated lumber for replacements to minimize future rot [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 252].

3. **Execution**
   - For replacement, we carefully remove the damaged post, ensuring that the structure is supported throughout the process. New posts are installed using metal post bases to elevate them above ground moisture, thus preventing rot [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 252].

4. **Quality Check & Cleanup**
   - After installation, we inspect the new posts for stability and alignment. All debris is cleared to leave your property clean and tidy.

5. **Follow-up**
   - We schedule a follow-up visit to ensure your new posts remain in optimal condition and discuss any maintenance tips.

## FAQ Section

1. **What causes deck posts to rot?**
   - Rot is primarily caused by prolonged moisture exposure. In Seattle, the damp climate accelerates this process. Regular inspections can help identify issues early.

2. **How do you replace a deck post?**
   - We assess the post's condition, support the deck, remove the damaged post, and install a new, pressure-treated post secured with metal connectors [Source: Charlie Wing, The Visual Handbook, Page 656].

3. **What materials do you recommend for deck posts?**
   - We recommend pressure-treated lumber for its resistance to moisture and rot, combined with metal post bases for stability [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 252].

4. **How long does the replacement process take?**
   - The timeline depends on the number of posts and the extent of repairs. Typically, the process takes a few hours to a day.

5. **Will my deck require any maintenance after the repair?**
   - Regular maintenance, including inspections for moisture and applying sealants, is recommended to prolong the life of your new posts.

6. **Can you repair a post without replacing it?**
   - Yes, smaller areas of rot can sometimes be repaired using epoxy or wood hardeners, depending on the extent of damage [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 250].

7. **What safety measures do you take during the repair?**
