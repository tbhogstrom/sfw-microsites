## Deck Ledger Board Replacement
Replacing a deck ledger board is a crucial task when maintaining the structural integrity of your deck, especially given the Pacific Northwest's notorious damp climate. The ledger board connects the deck to your home, and any decay or damage can lead to serious safety issues. Typically, a 2x8 or 2x10 ledger board is used, depending on the size and design of your deck<sup>1</sup>. When replacing the board, it's important to ensure that the new ledger is securely fastened and properly flashed to prevent water intrusion, which is a common problem in Portland due to frequent rain<sup>2</sup>. This process involves removing the old board, inspecting the area for any rot or damage, and ensuring that the new board is installed with high-grade fasteners and waterproofing materials. Proper installation and flashing are vital to prevent future water damage and to maintain the safety and longevity of your deck. If you suspect your deck ledger board needs replacement, it's essential to take action promptly to avoid further damage to your home and deck structure.

## Deck Ledger Repairs & Flashing
Proper deck ledger repairs and flashing are crucial for maintaining the structural integrity and weather resistance of your deck. In the Pacific Northwest, where rain and moisture are constant concerns, ensuring that your deck ledger board is securely attached and properly flashed can prevent significant water damage and rot. Deck ledger boards should be fastened using appropriate deck ties, such as DJT14 or DPT5, which provide the necessary support and protection against the elements<sup>3</sup>. Flashing, typically a metal or EPDM rubber membrane, is installed over the ledger board to direct water away and prevent it from seeping into the building envelope<sup>4</sup>. It's vital to ensure the flashing is installed with a proper lap and is secured with fasteners that have fender washers to prevent any gaps. Furthermore, the flashing should extend to cover the top of the ledger board and lap over the house wrap to channel water away from the structure. If your deck ledger or flashing is compromised, taking prompt action to repair it can save you from costly repairs down the road. Schedule an inspection today to ensure your deck is ready to weather the next storm.

## Deck Flashing Installation or Repair
Installing or repairing deck flashing is crucial for protecting your home from moisture damage, especially in Portland's wet climate. Proper deck flashing acts as a barrier, directing water away from the deck ledger board and preventing it from seeping into your home's structure. When installed correctly, flashing should tuck under the siding above and extend over the deck surface below, effectively channeling water outward<sup>5</sup>. Common issues with flashing include improper alignment and inadequate overlap, which can result in water infiltration and subsequent damage to the deck and home<sup>6</sup>. It's essential to ensure that the flashing is made from durable materials and correctly secured to withstand Pacific Northwest weather conditions. If you're noticing signs of water damage or suspect your deck flashing may not be up to par, it's time to take action. Consult with professionals who specialize in deck repairs to assess and enhance your deck's water protection system.

## Deck Waterproofing Repairs
In the damp climate of Portland, effective deck waterproofing is essential to protect your investment and ensure its longevity. Water can seep into wooden decks, leading to rot, mold, and structural damage over time. One common method to address deck waterproofing is the use of liquid membranes. These are either solvent-based or water-based systems, typically applied to a uniform thickness specified by the manufacturer. This ensures a seamless barrier against water infiltration<sup>7</sup>. Another effective solution is the use of self-adhering waterproof shingle underlayments (WSU). These can self-seal around nails, offering a robust defense against moisture intrusion<sup>8</sup>. If your deck is experiencing water damage, the first step is a professional inspection to assess the extent of the issue and discuss the best waterproofing solutions tailored to your specific needs. Don't wait until the damage worsens; schedule a waterproofing assessment today to protect your deck from the Pacific Northwest elements.

## Deck Drainage Corrections
Proper deck drainage is essential for maintaining the longevity and safety of your deck, especially in Portland's wet climate. Without effective drainage, water can pool on the deck surface or seep into the substructure, leading to rot and structural damage. Correcting drainage issues involves ensuring that water is directed away from the deck and its supports. A well-designed drainage system often includes sloping the deck surface slightly away from the house to facilitate runoff. Additionally, installing perforated pipes beneath the deck can help manage water drainage efficiently. These pipes should be positioned at a slope, with holes at the 4 and 8 o’clock positions to channel water away<sup>9</sup>. Another key component is the use of gravel and filter fabric around the drainage system to prevent soil and debris from clogging the pipes<sup>10</sup>. By addressing drainage problems promptly, you can protect your deck from water damage and extend its life. If you notice pooling water or suspect drainage issues, it’s time to explore your deck drainage correction options.
