## Deck Post Replacement
In Seattle's rainy climate, ensuring the stability of your deck is vital. Deck posts are the backbone of your deck structure, transferring loads to the ground. Replacing a deck post is crucial when you notice signs of rot, instability, or damage. Using pressure-treated lumber for new post installations is recommended to resist moisture and decay<sup>1</sup>. Begin by carefully removing the old post, ensuring the load is temporarily supported by jacks. Measure the required length for the new post, considering the height of the jack used during the replacement process<sup>2</sup>. Install post bases and caps to provide strong connections and prevent splitting, making the structure more durable. Reinforce the post's base with a metal plate and secure it with silicone caulking to prevent future rot. Once installed, lower the jack to transfer the load to the new post. If you're noticing signs of post failure, it's time to schedule an inspection to determine if a replacement is necessary.

## Footing Repairs & Replacement
Footings are the unsung heroes of your deck's structural integrity, especially in Seattle's damp climate. They serve as the foundation for deck posts, distributing the weight evenly to prevent settling and shifting. Over time, footings can degrade due to moisture, leading to instability. To ensure your deck remains safe and sturdy, footing repairs or replacements may be necessary. The repair process often involves assessing the existing footings and determining if they meet the latest building standards, such as ensuring they are below the frost line to prevent heaving<sup>3</sup>. If replacement is needed, footings should be poured with a minimum compressive strength of 2,500 psi to withstand Seattle's moderate to severe weather conditions<sup>4</sup>. Proper footing installation can prevent future issues and extend the life of your deck. If you suspect your deck's footings are compromised, it's crucial to address the issue before it leads to more significant problems. Schedule an inspection today to safeguard your deck's foundation.

## Deck Concrete Pier Repair
Concrete piers are a crucial component of your deck's foundation, especially in the Pacific Northwest where rain and moisture challenge structural integrity. If your deck's concrete piers are showing signs of wear or damage, such as cracking or settling, it's essential to address these issues promptly to maintain safety and stability. Repairing concrete piers involves several steps, including assessing the extent of the damage, removing any compromised material, and filling cracks with a high-strength epoxy, which can bond to both sides of the crack, providing a robust structural repair<sup>5</sup>. In cases where the damage is extensive, replacing the pier may be necessary. This involves lifting the deck with jacks, removing the damaged pier, and setting a new one below the frost line to prevent future movement<sup>6</sup>. For Seattle homeowners, these repairs are critical in ensuring your deck remains safe and functional throughout the rainy season. If your deck's concrete piers are compromised, schedule a professional assessment to determine the best course of action for repair or replacement.

## Deck Shoring & Safety
Ensuring the safety of your deck is crucial, especially in the damp climate of Seattle, where moisture can compromise structural integrity. Deck shoring involves reinforcing the deck's structure to prevent sagging or collapse, often using metal framing aids such as deck ties and rigid ties<sup>7</sup>. These components help to securely connect posts, beams, and joists, making the deck more stable and safe. Regular inspections are essential to identify any signs of wood rot or weakened connections that could pose safety hazards. If your deck is older or has been exposed to prolonged moisture, consider professional shoring services to reinforce weak spots before they become serious problems. In some cases, temporary shoring may be required to support the deck during repairs, ensuring safety while permanent solutions are implemented<sup>8</sup>. Take the proactive step of scheduling a deck inspection to determine if shoring is needed to maintain your deck's safety.

## Deck Leveling & Stabilization
In Seattle, where the Pacific Northwest weather can wreak havoc on outdoor structures, ensuring your deck is level and stable is crucial for both safety and longevity. Over time, weather conditions and soil movement can cause decks to become uneven, leading to structural weaknesses and safety hazards. Leveling and stabilization involve assessing the deck's current state, adjusting the support posts, and reinforcing footings. The process often includes using a spirit level to achieve a balanced deck surface and ensuring all components, such as joists and beams, are properly aligned<sup>9</sup>. Reinforcing footings below frost depth is essential to prevent future movement, especially in areas with significant rainfall<sup>10</sup>. If your deck shows signs of unevenness or instability, it's important to address these issues promptly to maintain a safe and functional outdoor space. Consult with our experts to evaluate your deck and determine the best course of action for leveling and stabilization.

## Deck Support Post Repair
Repairing deck support posts is crucial for maintaining the structural integrity of your deck, especially in the moist Pacific Northwest climate where wood rot is prevalent. Deck support posts can suffer from rot or damage due to prolonged exposure to moisture. When repairing these posts, it's essential to use pressure-treated lumber, as it offers enhanced resistance to decay and insect damage<sup>11</sup>. Start by assessing the condition of your current posts. If a post shows signs of significant rot or instability, it should be replaced. Cut the new post to fit snugly under the deck beam, ensuring it is plumb and adequately supported by a stable footing. For added protection against moisture, place a 22-gauge sheet aluminum plate beneath the post to act as a barrier<sup>12</sup>. Proper installation will prevent future issues and prolong the life of your deck. Don't let damaged support posts compromise your deck's safety—inspect and address issues promptly to ensure stability and safety.
