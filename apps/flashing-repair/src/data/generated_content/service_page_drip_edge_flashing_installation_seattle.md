## Service Overview

### What is Drip Edge Flashing Installation?

Drip edge flashing is a metal component installed along the edges of your roof, designed to direct water away from the roof and into the gutters. It helps to protect the underlying roof structure and prevents water from being drawn back up under the shingles due to capillary action [Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 94]. Our installation process involves carefully measuring, cutting, and securing the flashing to ensure it is properly aligned and sealed, preventing any potential leaks that could arise from improper installation.

Seattle's unique climate, characterized by frequent rain and high humidity, necessitates a robust roofing system that includes effective flashing. Without proper drip edge flashing, your home is susceptible to water damage, mold growth, and structural issues, especially during heavy downpours [Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 94]. Additionally, local building codes require the installation of drip edge flashing to ensure that homes are protected from the elements.

### Common Problems This Service Solves
- **Water Intrusion**: Without drip edge flashing, water can seep under shingles, leading to leaks and interior damage [Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 94].
- **Ice Dams**: Proper installation of drip edge flashing helps prevent ice dams, which can cause significant damage to roofing materials [Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 94].
