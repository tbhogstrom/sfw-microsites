## Service Overview

### What is Drip Edge Flashing Installation?

Drip edge flashing installation involves placing metal flashing along the eaves and rakes of a roof to channel water away from the roofing system. The installation process includes selecting the appropriate type of flashing—typically L-shaped or T-shaped—and integrating it seamlessly with the roofing materials. The flashing should extend beyond the edge of the roofing to effectively divert water and prevent it from seeping back into the structure [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 94].

### Why It's Important for Homeowners in Portland, Oregon

Given Portland's rainy climate, drip edge flashing is not just a recommendation but a necessity. Local building codes often require drip edge installation to mitigate risks associated with excessive moisture and water intrusion. Additionally, homes without effective drip edge flashing may face issues such as mold growth, wood rot, and structural damage due to prolonged exposure to water [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 94].

### Common Problems This Service Solves
- **Water Intrusion**: Without proper drip edge, water can be drawn back under shingles, leading to leaks [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 94].
- **Roof Damage**: Water pooling at roof edges can deteriorate shingles and underlayment, shortening the roof's lifespan [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 94].
- **Sheathing Damage**: Drip edges protect sheathing from gutter splashback and ice dams, common in Portland's damp conditions [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 94].
- **Aesthetic Issues**: A poorly finished roof edge can detract from the overall appearance of your home [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 94].
