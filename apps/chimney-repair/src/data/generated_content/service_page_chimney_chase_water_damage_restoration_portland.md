## Service Overview

### What is Chimney Chase Water Damage Restoration?

Chimney chase water damage restoration involves the inspection, repair, and prevention of water-related issues in chimney chases. This includes evaluating the integrity of the mortar joints, flashing, and chimney caps. Water intrusion can lead to significant problems such as mold growth, rotted framing, and structural instability, which can all be avoided with timely intervention [Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 21]. 

During our service, we assess the chimney chase for signs of water damage, such as crumbling mortar or missing flashing, and implement appropriate repairs to restore its functionality and prevent future issues.

The climate in Portland can be particularly challenging for chimney chases, with frequent rainfall and moisture accumulation leading to water damage. Local building codes necessitate that chimneys and their chases are correctly installed and maintained to prevent water intrusion and ensure safety [Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 21]. Regular maintenance and restoration services are essential for homeowners to protect their properties from costly repairs and ensure compliance with local regulations.

### Common Problems This Service Solves
- **Crumbling Mortar Joints**: Deteriorating mortar can lead to structural instability and water intrusion [Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 22].
- **Damaged Flashing**: Missing or degraded flashing can allow water to penetrate the chimney chase, causing extensive damage [Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 21].
- **Missing or Cracked Chimney Caps**: A compromised chimney cap can lead to water pooling and subsequent damage to the chimney structure [Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 21].
- **Improperly Installed Counterflashing**: Counterflashing that doesn’t shed water properly can create reservoirs of water, leading to leaks and damage [Source: Christina Glennon - Siding, Roofing, and Trim, Page 183].
