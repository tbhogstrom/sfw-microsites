## Service Overview

### What is Chimney Relining Services?

Chimney relining involves the installation of a new flue liner within an existing chimney structure. This can be done using various materials such as stainless steel, clay tile, or poured masonry, depending on the specific needs of your chimney and the type of fuel you use. A well-functioning liner protects your chimney from heat and corrosion, directs smoke and gases safely outside, and prevents combustible materials from igniting due to high temperatures [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 237].

In cases where the existing liner is cracked or damaged, relining is essential. It can be accomplished by inserting a new stainless-steel flue, installing a poured masonry liner, or utilizing a flexible liner that can adapt to the chimney's shape [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 236]. This process not only enhances safety but also improves the overall efficiency of your heating system.

### Common Problems This Service Solves
- **Cracked Flue Liners**: Flue tiles that are cracked allow superheated gases to escape, posing a fire hazard and potentially igniting adjacent framing [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 22].
- **Chimney Fires**: A buildup of creosote due to lack of maintenance can lead to chimney fires, which are often a result of inadequate flue linings [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 236].
- **Water Damage**: Deteriorated or missing liners can allow moisture to enter the chimney structure, causing damage to the masonry and creating unsafe conditions [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 237].
- **Code Compliance**: A chimney that lacks an appropriate liner is not compliant with local building codes, posing legal and safety issues for homeowners [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 236].
