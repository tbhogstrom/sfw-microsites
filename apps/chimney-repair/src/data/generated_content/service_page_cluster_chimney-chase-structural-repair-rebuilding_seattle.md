## Chimney Chase Repair and Rebuilding
Chimney chases in Seattle homes, especially those exposed to continuous rain and moisture, often require repair and rebuilding to ensure safety and functionality. The integrity of your chimney chase is crucial since it shields the structural framing and flue from the elements. Over time, rainwater can penetrate compromised mortar joints or poorly installed crowns, leading to extensive water damage and potential rot within the chase structure<sup>1</sup>. Addressing these issues starts with a thorough inspection to assess the extent of the damage. Mortar joints that are solid but slightly worn can be repointed, while more severe erosion might necessitate a complete rebuild of the chase<sup>2</sup>. Additionally, ensuring that the chimney cap or crown is properly constructed with an overhang can prevent water from dripping onto the chimney face, further protecting the structure from water damage. If you suspect your chimney chase needs attention, consider having a professional evaluation to determine the best course of action.

## Chimney Chase Structural Framing Repair
When it comes to chimney chase structural framing repair, Seattle homeowners must be particularly vigilant due to the region's heavy rainfall and older housing stock. The structural framing of your chimney chase supports not only the chimney itself but also prevents water intrusion into your home. Over time, moisture can cause wood framing to rot, compromising the integrity of your chimney chase and potentially leading to leaks or even structural failure<sup>3</sup>. It’s essential that any repair includes replacing damaged wood with new, pressure-treated lumber to ensure durability against future moisture exposure<sup>4</sup>. SFW Construction specializes in identifying and repairing such issues, ensuring that your chimney chase remains both functional and aesthetically pleasing. If you suspect any damage to your chimney chase framing, act promptly to prevent further deterioration and protect your home.

## Chimney Chase Reframing & Reinforcement
In the Pacific Northwest, the frequent rain and moisture can quickly deteriorate a chimney chase, leading to structural issues that require reframing and reinforcement. A strong, well-constructed chimney chase is essential to prevent water intrusion and protect the integrity of your home. The reframing process involves installing a new frame using durable materials like pressure-treated wood, which resists rot and withstands the region's wet conditions. Reinforcement is achieved by incorporating additional bracing and using high-quality flashing around the chimney to ensure water is effectively diverted away from the structure<sup>5</sup>. Proper flashing installation, including step flashing and counterflashing, is crucial to prevent leaks that can lead to rot and other damage<sup>6</sup>. If your chimney chase is showing signs of wear or water damage, it's time to consider reframing and reinforcement to maintain the safety and functionality of your chimney. Contact our team to schedule a comprehensive inspection and protect your home from further damage.

## Wooden Chimney Chase Framing Repair
Wooden chimney chases in Seattle homes can suffer significant damage due to the region's persistent rain and moisture, leading to rot and structural weakness<sup>7</sup>. Often clad in siding, a wooden chimney chase can disguise internal damage until the deterioration reaches critical levels. Repairing the wooden framing of a chimney chase involves removing the damaged sections and replacing them with new, treated lumber to ensure longevity<sup>8</sup>. Additionally, it is crucial to inspect the chimney's flashing, as improper or worn-out flashing can allow water to seep into the frame, exacerbating decay<sup>7</sup>. Proper flashing involves using L-shaped pieces that are overlapped and fitted tightly to shed water efficiently<sup>8</sup>. After repairs, the chimney chase should be resealed and painted with weather-resistant materials to protect against further moisture intrusion. If your home's chimney chase has signs of damage or you're uncertain about its condition, it's time to schedule an inspection to prevent further deterioration.

## Chimney Chase Top Rebuild
A chimney chase top rebuild is crucial for maintaining the structural integrity of your chimney, especially in Seattle's damp climate. Over time, moisture can cause the mortar joints of the chimney chase top to deteriorate, leading to leaks and potential structural damage<sup>9</sup>. Rebuilding the chase top involves removing the old, damaged materials and installing a new, durable concrete crown that overhangs the chimney by at least 1 to 1.5 inches. This design effectively redirects water away from the chimney structure, preventing the infiltration of rain and snow<sup>10</sup>. The process also includes sealing any gaps with urethane caulk to ensure a watertight finish. A properly rebuilt chimney chase top not only protects against water damage but also enhances the overall safety and lifespan of your chimney. If your chimney chase top shows signs of wear, schedule a professional evaluation to prevent further damage and costly repairs.

## Chimney Chase Framing Replacement
Replacing the framing of a chimney chase is crucial to maintaining the structural integrity of your home, especially in the damp, rainy climate of Seattle. Over time, the wooden framing can become compromised due to water infiltration and rot, leading to structural weaknesses that may even pose safety hazards. When we undertake a chimney chase framing replacement, we first remove the compromised materials, ensuring that any decayed wood is replaced with new, pressure-treated lumber. This type of lumber is resistant to moisture, which is vital for preventing future rot and damage<sup>11</sup>. Additionally, proper flashing is installed to protect the new framing from water intrusion. The flashing should be tightly secured around the chimney to direct water away, thereby preventing leaks<sup>12</sup>. With the right materials and techniques, a chimney chase framing replacement can extend the life of your chimney and protect your home from water damage. If you notice signs of rot or water damage around your chimney chase, it's time to consider a framing replacement before the next rainy season begins.
