## Chimney Chase Waterproofing & Sealing
Protecting your chimney chase from water intrusion is vital for maintaining the structural integrity of your home. In Portland's damp climate, moisture can easily penetrate untreated masonry, leading to costly damage. Exterior sealants, categorized as water-repellent and waterproofing coatings, allow masonry to breathe while preventing water from seeping in<sup>1</sup>. It's crucial to apply a high-quality sealant that accommodates the unique conditions of the Pacific Northwest to shield your chimney chase effectively. Regular inspections can identify cracks or gaps in mortar joints or flue tiles, which can compromise the chase if not addressed<sup>2</sup>. By sealing these vulnerabilities, you not only extend the life of your chimney but also protect your home from potential water damage. If it's been a while since your chimney chase was last sealed, consider scheduling an inspection to ensure it remains in top condition.

## Chimney Chase Vent Penetration Sealing
In the Pacific Northwest, where moisture is a constant concern, ensuring your chimney chase is properly sealed is crucial. Water penetration around vent pipes can lead to significant damage, including rot and mold, which can compromise the structural integrity of your chimney and surrounding areas. To combat this, it's essential to seal any gaps around vent penetrations effectively. Start by using high-temperature caulk to fill the spaces between the vent pipe and the chimney structure<sup>3</sup>. This creates a durable, water-resistant barrier that can withstand the region's wet climate. Additionally, galvanized steel flashing should be installed around the vent pipes, secured with roofing nails, to provide further protection against water ingress<sup>4</sup>. Regular inspections and maintenance are key to ensuring long-term performance. If you notice any signs of water damage or suspect that your vent penetration seal is failing, it's time to take action. Schedule a professional inspection to assess the condition of your chimney chase vent sealing today.

## Chimney Chase Cap Replacement
Replacing a chimney chase cap is essential to maintaining the integrity of your chimney system, particularly in the rainy climate of Portland, Oregon. A damaged or missing chase cap can allow water to seep into the chimney, leading to structural damage, mold growth, and rot. When replacing a chimney chase cap, it's vital to choose durable materials that can withstand the Pacific Northwest's harsh weather conditions. Stainless steel or copper caps are recommended due to their corrosion resistance and longevity<sup>5</sup>. An effective cap should be slightly elevated and designed to shed water away from the chimney, preventing pooling and subsequent damage<sup>6</sup>. If your chimney has multiple flue liners, you may need a custom cap to ensure complete protection. Neglecting this crucial component can result in costly repairs down the line, so consider upgrading your chimney chase cap before the next rainy season sets in. Contact us today to discuss your options for a durable and effective chimney chase cap replacement.

## Chimney Chase Rot Repair
Dealing with rot in your chimney chase can be a serious issue, particularly in the damp climate of Portland, Oregon. Rot typically occurs when water penetrates the chase, causing wood to deteriorate. This is often due to missing or degraded flashing, which allows moisture to seep into the structure<sup>7</sup>. To address this problem, it's essential to remove the decayed wood and replace it with new, solid materials. Ensuring that the flashing is properly installed and that all joints are sealed with a high-quality urethane caulk can prevent future water intrusion<sup>8</sup>. Additionally, the use of treated lumber for structural components can provide an extra layer of protection against decay. Regular inspections and maintenance are crucial to catching any signs of rot early, before they lead to more extensive and costly repairs. If you suspect rot in your chimney chase, taking swift action to repair and seal the area is key to maintaining the structural integrity of your home.

To protect your home from further damage, schedule a professional inspection today to assess and address any potential rot issues in your chimney chase.
