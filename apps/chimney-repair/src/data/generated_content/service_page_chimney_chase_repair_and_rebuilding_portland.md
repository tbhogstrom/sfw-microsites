## Service Overview

### What is Chimney Chase Repair and Rebuilding?

Chimney chase repair and rebuilding involves inspecting, repairing, or completely reconstructing the chimney chase that encapsulates your flue system. This process may include replacing damaged or missing chimney caps, repairing crumbling mortar joints, and ensuring that the flashing is properly installed to prevent water intrusion [Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 237]. Additionally, it may involve relining the flue with stainless steel or poured masonry to ensure safe venting of gases and smoke [Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 237].

### Why It's Important for Homeowners in Portland, Oregon

Portland’s climate, characterized by significant rainfall and moisture, can lead to rapid deterioration of chimney chases. The constant exposure to damp conditions may result in cracking, warping, and other structural issues that compromise the integrity of your chimney system. The local building codes necessitate proper chimney construction and maintenance to ensure safety and efficiency, making timely repairs crucial for any homeowner in the area.

### Common Problems This Service Solves
- **Cracked or Missing Flue Tiles**: These allow superheated gases to escape, potentially igniting surrounding materials [Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 22].
- **Ineffective Water Drainage**: Poorly constructed chase caps lead to water pooling, which can result in significant damage [Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 236].
- **Deteriorated Mortar Joints**: Eroded mortar can lead to structural instability and increased risk of chimney fires [Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 21].
- **Inadequate Flashing**: Failed flashing can cause leaks and water damage to your home [Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 236].
