## Service Overview

### What is Home Mold Risk Assessment?

A Home Mold Risk Assessment involves a detailed inspection of your property to identify mold growth and assess moisture levels that contribute to mold development. The process includes visual inspections, moisture readings with specialized equipment, and air quality testing. Our trained professionals follow systematic procedures to ensure comprehensive coverage of your property. If mold is detected, we provide detailed reports on its location, type, and recommendations for remediation, ensuring you are fully informed about the health risks associated with mold exposure [Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 415].

Seattle's unique climate—characterized by high humidity and significant rainfall—creates an environment conducive to mold growth. By investing in a Home Mold Risk Assessment, Seattle homeowners can identify potential mold issues before they escalate, protecting both their health and property value.

## Our Process

### Step-by-Step Breakdown

1. **Initial Assessment**: We begin with a thorough visual inspection of your home to identify visible mold and signs of moisture. Areas such as basements, attics, and bathrooms are prioritized due to their susceptibility to mold growth.

2. **Planning & Preparation**: If mold is suspected, we prepare a detailed plan for testing, which may include obtaining necessary permits and selecting appropriate materials for remediation if required.

3. **Execution**: Using professional-grade moisture meters, we assess humidity levels in various areas of your home. If mold is detected, we utilize HEPA-filtered vacuums and other specialized equipment to contain and remove mold safely [Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 415].

4. **Quality Check & Cleanup**: After remediation, we conduct a quality check to ensure all mold has been safely removed and that moisture levels are within acceptable limits.

5. **Follow-up**: We provide a follow-up consultation to discuss the findings and recommend preventive measures to minimize future mold risks.
