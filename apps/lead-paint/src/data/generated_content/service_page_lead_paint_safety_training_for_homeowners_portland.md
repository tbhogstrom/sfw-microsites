## Service Overview

### What is Lead Paint Safety Training for Homeowners?

Lead Paint Safety Training for Homeowners is a comprehensive program designed to educate individuals about the dangers of lead-based paint commonly found in homes built before 1978. This training covers various aspects, including identification of lead paint, safe handling practices, and the legal requirements set by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA). Participants will learn about lead dust hazards, safe work practices, and disposal methods for lead-contaminated materials, ensuring compliance with the EPA's Renovation, Repair, and Painting (RRP) Rule [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 524].

## Our Process

### Step-by-Step Breakdown

1. **Initial Assessment**: We begin with a thorough evaluation of your home to identify areas where lead paint may be present. This includes visual inspections and testing for lead using kits or professional services.

2. **Planning & Preparation**: Once lead sources are identified, we assist homeowners in planning safe renovation practices. This includes obtaining necessary permits and selecting appropriate materials and techniques.

3. **Execution**: During the training, participants learn safe work practices such as using HEPA-filter vacuums and proper personal protective equipment (PPE) to minimize lead dust exposure [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 524].

4. **Quality Check & Cleanup**: After renovations, it's vital to ensure the work area is free of lead dust. Our training covers effective cleanup methods, including proper disposal of lead-contaminated waste to comply with EPA regulations [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 524].

5. **Follow-up**: We provide resources for ongoing education and a checklist for maintaining lead-safe practices in your home, ensuring long-term safety.
