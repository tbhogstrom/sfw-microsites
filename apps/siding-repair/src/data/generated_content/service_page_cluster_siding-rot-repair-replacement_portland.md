## Siding Rot Repair
Siding rot is a common issue in Portland due to the Pacific Northwest's damp climate, which makes it crucial to address rot promptly to prevent further damage. The first step in siding rot repair is identifying the affected areas. Use a pocketknife or awl to gently probe discolored or soft sections of the siding. This will help determine the extent of rot beneath the surface<sup>1</sup>. Once identified, the damaged siding must be removed carefully to avoid harming the weather-resistive barrier, such as housewrap, that lies underneath<sup>2</sup>. After removing the rotten portions, you can replace them with new siding that matches the existing material. Ensure that the new siding is treated with a water repellent to extend its lifespan and protect against future moisture damage<sup>1</sup>. Regular maintenance, such as repainting or staining, will also help preserve the integrity of the siding. If you're noticing signs of rot on your siding, it's time to take action and protect your home from further damage by scheduling a professional assessment and repair.

## Dry Rot Siding Replacement
Dry rot is a common issue for Portland homes, especially given the region's wet climate. This type of decay occurs in wood that has been exposed to moisture over time, which can compromise the structural integrity of your home’s siding. Replacing siding affected by dry rot is crucial to maintaining your home’s durability. When addressing dry rot, it's important to assess the extent of the damage. Look for signs such as discoloration, a musty smell, or wood that feels spongy when touched<sup>3</sup>. Once identified, the damaged siding needs to be carefully removed and replaced with new materials. It's often advisable to select materials that are more resistant to moisture, such as fiber cement siding, which offers better dimensional stability and lower maintenance requirements<sup>4</sup>. A thorough inspection of the underlying structure is also essential to ensure no hidden damage persists. By tackling dry rot promptly, you can prevent further deterioration and protect your home’s value. If you suspect dry rot in your siding, it’s time to schedule an inspection and discuss replacement options with a qualified professional.

## Water Damaged Siding Repair
Water damage is a common issue for Portland homeowners, given the region's frequent rainfall. When water penetrates your siding, it can lead to problems like swelling, warping, and eventual rot if not addressed promptly. The first step in repairing water-damaged siding is to assess the extent of the damage. Look for signs such as discoloration, softness, or mold growth. In many cases, damaged wood and fiber-cement siding can be repaired in place without the need for complete replacement<sup>5</sup>. Repairs often involve removing the affected sections, treating the area to prevent further moisture infiltration, and sealing with a weather-resistant barrier. It's crucial to ensure that the weather-resistive barrier, such as housewrap, is intact and performing well, as it plays a vital role in directing water away from your home<sup>6</sup>. By addressing water damage early, you can prevent more severe structural issues down the line. If you suspect water damage in your siding, schedule an inspection to determine the best course of action.

## Siding Board Replacement
Replacing siding boards is a common necessity for Portland homeowners dealing with the Pacific Northwest's wet climate. Over time, moisture exposure can cause wood siding to warp, rot, or crack, compromising your home's protection against the elements. When replacing siding boards, it's crucial to use rust-resistant nails such as stainless steel or hot-dipped galvanized to prevent corrosion, especially in our damp environment<sup>7</sup>. This ensures a longer-lasting installation and helps avoid future repairs. Removing damaged boards requires careful handling to avoid harming the housewrap beneath, which acts as a critical moisture barrier<sup>8</sup>. Once the damaged boards are removed, install the new boards ensuring they are securely fastened and properly aligned with existing siding. Be sure to treat both sides of the new siding with a water repellent before installation to maximize durability and resistance to moisture. If your siding boards are showing signs of damage, contact us today to discuss your specific repair needs and protect your home from further weather-related issues.

## Siding Panel Replacement
Replacing siding panels is a crucial maintenance task for homeowners in the Pacific Northwest, where persistent rain and moisture can lead to significant wear and tear. Vinyl siding is commonly used due to its ease of replacement and durability. Each vinyl panel is designed with a nailing strip along the top and an interlocking edge at the bottom, facilitating straightforward removal and installation<sup>9</sup>. If damage occurs, a vinyl-siding removal tool is used to unlock the bottom edge of the panel. Once freed, the nailing strip is exposed, allowing the nails to be removed so the damaged panel can be replaced with a new one<sup>9</sup>. It is important to handle siding panels carefully, as improper removal can damage the weather-resistive barrier, such as housewrap, which plays a critical role in protecting your home from water infiltration<sup>10</sup>. Ensuring that your siding panels are properly installed not only maintains the aesthetic appeal of your home but also contributes to its structural integrity. For effective replacement of damaged siding panels, rely on local experts who understand the unique challenges of Portland's climate. ## Rotted Siding Framing Repair
Rotted siding framing is a common issue in the Pacific Northwest due to the region's persistent rain and moisture. When moisture penetrates siding and reaches the framing, it can lead to wood rot, compromising the structural integrity of your home. The first step in repairing rotted siding framing is to identify and remove all damaged areas. This involves carefully prying off the affected siding to expose the underlying framing<sup>11</sup>. Once exposed, the rotted sections of the wood framing must be cut out and replaced with new, treated lumber that resists moisture and decay. Ensuring proper installation is crucial; the new framing should be securely fastened and aligned with existing structures to maintain the wall's strength. It's also important to address the source of moisture intrusion, whether it's poor drainage, inadequate flashing, or damaged housewrap, to prevent future rot<sup>12</sup>. After the repair, the siding is reinstalled, and the seams are sealed to protect against water infiltration. To safeguard your home from further damage, consider having a professional inspection to assess the extent of the rot and implement comprehensive repair solutions.

Take action now by scheduling a professional inspection to determine the extent of your siding framing damage and protect your home's structural integrity.

## Wall Framing Rot Repair Behind Siding
Wall framing rot behind siding is a significant issue for many homeowners in the Pacific Northwest, where persistent moisture can easily infiltrate older homes. This problem often results from improper drainage or siding that sits too close to the ground, allowing water to seep into the framing<sup>13</sup>. When moisture penetrates the siding, it can lead to rot in the wooden frame, compromising the structural integrity of your home. Identifying rot early is crucial; you can do this by probing suspect areas with a tool such as an awl. If the awl penetrates easily, rot is likely present<sup>14</sup>.

Repairing wall framing rot involves removing the damaged sections and replacing them with sound, pressure-treated wood that resists future decay. It's essential to assess the extent of the damage before starting repairs, as rot can sometimes spread further than initially visible. Once the repairs are complete, ensuring proper ventilation and drainage around the siding will help prevent further issues. If you suspect your home may have wall framing rot behind the siding, it's time to schedule a professional inspection to address the problem before it worsens.
