## Service Overview

### What is Siding Installation for New Construction?

Siding installation for new construction involves applying protective and decorative materials to the exterior of a new building. This service includes selecting appropriate siding materials, preparing the surface, and ensuring that the installation adheres to local building codes. Key aspects of the installation process include:

- **Nailing Patterns**: Following recommended nailing patterns is crucial for stability. For instance, nails should penetrate framing members by at least 1.5 inches for optimal hold [Source: Charlie Wing, Page 244].
- **Proper Overlap**: Siding courses should overlap correctly to prevent water intrusion, with a minimum overlap of 1 inch between each course [Source: Charlie Wing, Page 244].
- **Moisture Management**: Incorporating air and water barriers during installation is essential to protect against moisture buildup [Source: Charlie Wing, Page 243].
- **Material Selection**: Choosing the right materials—be it wood, vinyl, or fiber-cement—tailored to Seattle's climate ensures longevity and performance.
