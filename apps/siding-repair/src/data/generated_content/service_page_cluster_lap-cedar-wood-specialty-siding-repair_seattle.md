## Lap Siding Repair
Lap siding is a popular choice for many Seattle homeowners due to its durability and aesthetic appeal. However, the Pacific Northwest's rainy climate can lead to issues such as water intrusion, rot, and weathering over time. To address these issues, it's crucial to identify the method of attachment and carefully remove damaged siding without harming the underlying weather-resistive barrier<sup>1</sup>. Repairing lap siding often involves using corrosion-resistant nails and ensuring proper overlap and alignment to maintain a watertight seal<sup>2</sup>. Seattle's moisture can cause wood to expand and contract, so it's important to allow for slight movement by maintaining appropriate gaps and using caulking where necessary. If you notice signs of damage like cracking or discoloration, it might be time to inspect your siding for necessary repairs. Our team at SFW Construction is ready to help you prolong the life of your lap siding with expert repairs tailored to the local climate. Start by scheduling a thorough inspection to prevent further damage.

## Cedar Siding Repair
Cedar siding is a popular choice among Seattle homeowners for its natural beauty and durability, but even cedar isn't immune to the dampness prevalent in the Pacific Northwest. Over time, exposure to moisture can cause cedar siding to develop rot, splits, or discoloration. Repairing cedar siding requires careful attention to detail to ensure that the repair blends seamlessly with the existing siding. When repairing, it's crucial to use a suitable primer, such as an alkyd oil primer, to prevent tannin bleed-through—a common issue with cedar<sup>3</sup>. This type of primer helps seal the wood and prepares it for finishing. Additionally, any damaged sections should be replaced with new cedar that matches the existing siding in grain and texture, ensuring a cohesive appearance. Properly primed and finished cedar siding can withstand the elements and maintain its appearance for years<sup>4</sup>. If you're noticing signs of damage in your cedar siding, such as visible rot or peeling paint, it's time to consider a professional repair to protect your home and preserve its curb appeal.

## Wood Siding Repair
Wood siding is a popular choice among Seattle homeowners due to its natural beauty and durability. However, the Pacific Northwest's moist climate can lead to issues like rot, warping, and cracking if not properly maintained. Regular inspections are crucial for early detection of damage, particularly in areas exposed to intense weather conditions like the south-facing walls<sup>5</sup>. Repairing wood siding often involves replacing individual damaged boards, but minor issues such as gouges or splits can sometimes be repaired in place without full replacement<sup>6</sup>. Ensuring the use of corrosion-resistant nails, such as stainless steel or hot-dipped galvanized, is essential to prevent premature deterioration<sup>5</sup>. Additionally, treating both sides of the siding with a water repellent before installation can significantly reduce moisture-related problems<sup>6</sup>. If your wood siding is showing signs of damage, it's time to act swiftly to prevent further deterioration. Contact us, and our experienced team will assess and repair your wood siding to protect your home against the elements.

## Siding Seam Repair
Siding seams are crucial for maintaining the integrity of your home's exterior, especially in Seattle's damp climate. Over time, seams can become compromised due to moisture infiltration, leading to potential water damage. If your home's siding seams are showing signs of wear, it's essential to address the issue promptly to prevent more significant problems. When repairing siding seams, it's important to ensure that the joint treatments are properly executed. For instance, caulk should be applied between plank ends to create a moderate contact that prevents water penetration<sup>7</sup>. Additionally, using joint flashing can further protect seams by directing water away from vulnerable areas. A well-executed repair will typically involve removing any damaged sections, securing the new siding panels, and ensuring a tight seal with the appropriate caulking and flashing techniques<sup>8</sup>. Don't let damaged siding seams compromise your home's defense against Seattle's relentless rain. Schedule a professional inspection today to safeguard your property.
