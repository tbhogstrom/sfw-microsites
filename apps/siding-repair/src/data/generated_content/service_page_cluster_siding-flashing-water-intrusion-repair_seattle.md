## Siding Flashing Repair
Flashing is a critical component in protecting your Seattle home from water damage, especially with the Pacific Northwest's persistent rainfall. Properly installed flashing should direct water away from your home’s siding, helping to prevent leaks and moisture intrusion<sup>1</sup>. However, over time, flashing can deteriorate due to age, improper installation, or damage from weather, leading to potential siding issues. Repairs typically involve resealing or replacing the flashing to ensure it directs water away effectively. This involves sliding new flashing beneath the siding and securing it with stainless steel or galvanized nails to prevent corrosion<sup>2</sup>. If you notice signs of water damage or suspect flashing issues, it's crucial to address them promptly to protect your home’s structural integrity. Ensure your home stays dry and protected by having our experts evaluate and repair your siding flashing today.

## Siding Leak Repair
Experiencing leaks in your siding can be a significant concern for Seattle homeowners, especially with the region's heavy rainfall and damp climate. Water intrusion through siding often occurs when barriers like housewraps or felt paper, designed to protect your home, are compromised<sup>3</sup>. Over time, wood movement, heavy rain, and other factors can create gaps and cracks, allowing water to seep through<sup>4</sup>. The key to effective siding leak repair is to identify and address these weaknesses promptly. 

Start by inspecting your siding for visible signs of damage, such as cracks or loose panels. It's crucial to ensure that the weather-resistive barriers are intact and properly overlapped to direct water away from the house structure<sup>3</sup>. If you find any gaps, resealing them with appropriate caulking or replacing damaged sections can prevent further water ingress. Remember, even minor leaks can lead to significant issues if left unattended, so it's best to address them early to avoid costly repairs down the line. 

If you're unsure about the extent of the damage or the best repair approach, schedule a professional siding inspection to safeguard your home against future leaks.

## Siding Water Intrusion Repair
Water intrusion is a common problem for Seattle homeowners due to the region's abundant rainfall. When water finds its way behind siding, it can cause significant damage, including wood rot and mold. The key to preventing these issues lies in proper siding installation and maintenance. Siding acts as the first line of defense against moisture, but it is not waterproof on its own. Gaps, cracks, and improper installation can allow water to penetrate, which is why it’s crucial that the siding is complemented by a weather-resistive barrier (WRB) such as housewrap<sup>5</sup>. This barrier works in tandem with the siding to repel water effectively.

Repairs are necessary when the WRB is compromised or when siding is damaged. A critical step in repair is ensuring that all joints are properly sealed with caulk and that any damaged sections are replaced promptly. Flashing at horizontal junctures should also be inspected and repaired to direct water away from the structure<sup>6</sup>. To prevent further damage and protect your home, consider scheduling a water intrusion inspection today.

## Siding & Roofline Repair
Repairing the siding and roofline of your Seattle home is crucial to protecting against the relentless Pacific Northwest weather. The siding and roofline are integral to your home's defense against rain and moisture, which are prevalent in this region<sup>7</sup>. Over time, rain and wind can cause damage to these areas, leading to issues such as water intrusion and structural wear. Addressing these repairs promptly is essential to maintaining your home's integrity and preventing further damage. 

Siding repair often involves replacing damaged sections or patching areas with housewrap and flashing to ensure a watertight seal<sup>8</sup>. Meanwhile, roofline repairs may include fixing or replacing fascia boards, soffits, or gutter systems that have been compromised. Using durable materials and correct installation techniques can extend the life of these components and improve your home's resilience to weather. 

If you're noticing signs of damage, like peeling paint, rot, or mildew, it's time to act. Reach out to us to schedule a professional assessment of your siding and roofline, and secure your home against Seattle's challenging weather.
