## Service Overview

### What is Roof Inspection and Maintenance?

Roof inspection and maintenance involve a thorough examination of your roofing system, identifying signs of damage or wear that could lead to leaks or structural issues. This process includes checking for missing shingles, inspecting flashing around chimneys and vents, and assessing the overall condition of the roof deck. Our team employs specialized tools and techniques to ensure a comprehensive evaluation, following best practices outlined in professional construction handbooks [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 19].

### Why It's Important for Homeowners in Seattle, Washington

Seattle's unique climate presents specific challenges for roofing systems. The frequent rainfall, coupled with occasional snow and ice, can lead to water damage if roofs are not properly maintained. Ensuring your roof is inspected and maintained regularly helps prevent water infiltration that can compromise your home’s structural integrity. Moreover, local building codes may require specific maintenance practices to adhere to safety regulations [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 18].

### Common Problems This Service Solves
- **Water Damage**: Regular inspections can help identify leaks before they cause extensive damage [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 19].
- **Poor Drainage**: Lack of gutters or improper drainage can lead to significant foundation issues, especially in older homes [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 18].
- **Structural Issues**: Identifying sagging or damaged rafters early can prevent larger structural repairs [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 19].
- **Deteriorated Flashing**: Checking and replacing worn flashing can prevent leaks at vulnerable points on the roof [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 19].
