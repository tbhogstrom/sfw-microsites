## Post & Column Repair
Post and column repair is a vital service for maintaining the structural integrity of homes in Portland, where the combination of wet weather and older homes often results in damage to these critical components. Over time, moisture can lead to wood rot, weakening wooden posts and columns to the point where they no longer support the loads they were designed to bear. This can cause sagging floors and compromised structural integrity. For effective repairs, it's crucial to properly support the building before removing any damaged posts or columns. Temporary shoring is recommended, using hydraulic jacks to carefully lift and stabilize the structure<sup>1</sup>. Once secured, damaged posts can be replaced with pressure-treated lumber or even preprimed metal columns to prevent future rot<sup>2</sup>. Ensuring the new posts are correctly installed with metal connectors like Simpson Strong-Tie anchors can provide additional stability and prolong the life of the repair. If you suspect your home's posts or columns need attention, don't wait for the problem to worsen; start by assessing the condition of your supports to determine the necessary repairs.

## Support Post Repair
When it comes to support post repair in Portland, the frequent rain and moisture can lead to wood rot and structural issues in older homes. Identifying early signs of damage, such as sloping floors or sticking doors, is critical to prevent further deterioration. The repair process begins by carefully lifting the structure using hydraulic jacks, ensuring even pressure is applied to maintain stability<sup>3</sup>. Once elevated, the damaged support post can be removed. It is often replaced with pressure-treated lumber or a preprimed metal column to resist future rot and moisture<sup>4</sup>. To secure the new post, steel caps are nailed to the tops of shoring posts, which helps in stabilizing the beam during the repair. This meticulous approach ensures a safe and lasting repair, restoring structural integrity to your home. If you suspect your support posts may be compromised, schedule a professional inspection to address the issue before it worsens.

## Support Post Replacement
Replacing support posts in your Portland home is crucial for maintaining structural integrity, especially in the Pacific Northwest where moisture can accelerate wood rot. The process begins with careful removal of the existing post. First, use a hydraulic jack to slightly lift the weight off the post<sup>5</sup>. Once the load is relieved, the old post can be removed safely. It's essential to measure accurately from the base pad to the girder to cut a new post to the correct height. Using pressure-treated lumber is recommended to resist decay<sup>5</sup>.

For added protection against moisture, place a 22-gauge sheet aluminum plate beneath the post to prevent rot<sup>6</sup>. A dab of silicone caulking will ensure the plate stays in place during installation. After positioning the new post, carefully lower the jack, allowing the structure to rest securely on the new support. If your basement is prone to dampness, consider replacing wooden posts with preprimed metal columns for increased durability<sup>6</sup>.

Ensure your home's structural safety by opting for professional support post replacement tailored to Portland's unique climate challenges.

## Structural Post Replacement
Replacing structural posts in your home is crucial for maintaining the integrity of your building's support system, especially in the Pacific Northwest where older homes and moisture can lead to deterioration. When replacing a structural post, it is essential to ensure that the load is properly transferred to the new post. This involves using temporary shoring to support the weight of the structure during the replacement process. Shoring posts should be positioned a few inches away from jacking posts, and as you jack, aim to raise both ends of the beam evenly using a spirit level to maintain balance<sup>7</sup>. Once the old post is removed, measure from the underside of the girder to the pad, subtracting a minimal amount to ensure a snug fit, and cut a new post from pressure-treated lumber or consider upgrading to a preprimed metal column to prevent future rot<sup>8</sup>. Properly securing the new post with metal connectors like Simpson Strong-Tie anchors will help stabilize it and distribute loads effectively. If you're concerned about the condition of your structural posts or need assistance with a replacement, schedule an inspection with our experienced team to ensure your home's safety.

## Load-Bearing Post Repair
Load-bearing post repair is crucial for maintaining the structural integrity of your Pacific Northwest home, especially in the damp climate of Portland. Over time, moisture can cause wood posts to rot, compromising their ability to support loads effectively. When addressing load-bearing post issues, it's important to first relieve the existing post of any load using hydraulic jacks and shoring posts, ensuring the structure remains stable during repairs<sup>9</sup>. Once the load is transferred, the damaged post can be safely removed. Often, replacing a rotted wooden post with a pressure-treated one or even a preprimed metal column is recommended to prevent future issues<sup>10</sup>. Properly securing the new post with metal connectors and ensuring a continuous load path down to the foundation is essential for long-term stability. If you notice any sagging or instability in your floors, act quickly to assess and repair your load-bearing posts to avoid more extensive structural damage.

## Wood Post Rot Repair
Wood posts in your home's structure are particularly vulnerable to rot, especially in the damp climate of Portland. Moisture can wick up through concrete pads, leading to the deterioration of the post's base<sup>11</sup>. When addressing rot, it's essential to cut back to sound wood to prevent future problems. Using pressure-treated lumber for replacements can help prevent rot from moisture exposure. Additionally, placing a 22-gauge sheet aluminum plate under the post can further protect it from ground moisture<sup>12</sup>. If you're dealing with rot, it's crucial to assess the extent of the damage thoroughly and replace the affected wood to ensure the structural integrity of your home. ## Steel Support Post Installation
Steel support post installation is a crucial aspect of structural repair, especially in the Pacific Northwest, where heavy rainfall can compromise the integrity of older homes. Steel posts, often referred to as columns, provide robust support for beams and help distribute loads effectively to the foundation. Before installation, it's essential to consult with a structural engineer to determine the appropriate size and type of steel post required for your specific needs<sup>13</sup>. The installation process typically involves securing the post to both the beam and the foundation using steel connectors, such as post caps or base plates, which prevent lateral movement and ensure stability<sup>14</sup>. It's important to ensure that the posts are plumb and that all connectors are tightly fastened to maintain the structural integrity of the building. If you're dealing with compromised posts or planning a renovation, consider installing steel support posts to safeguard your home's structural health for years to come. ## Adjustable Post Installation
Installing adjustable posts is an effective solution for homeowners dealing with sagging floors or uneven support in older homes, common issues in Portland's damp climate. These posts, which can be precisely adjusted to the desired height, help in leveling and stabilizing structures that might have settled over time due to moisture-related wood rot or shifting foundations. For installation, first, ensure the area is clear and safe. Then, using a hydraulic jack, carefully lift the beam or joist to the required height, ensuring it's level with a spirit level<sup>15</sup>. Once the beam is at the correct height, place the adjustable post beneath it, ensuring it is plumb. Secure the post with metal connectors like Simpson Strong-Tie anchors to prevent lateral movement and ensure stability<sup>16</sup>. Once in place, these posts can be adjusted as needed to accommodate changes in the home's structure over time. This adaptability makes them an ideal choice for homeowners looking to maintain the integrity of their homes.
