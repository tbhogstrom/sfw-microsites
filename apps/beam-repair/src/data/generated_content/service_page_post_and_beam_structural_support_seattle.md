## Service Overview

### What is Post and Beam Structural Support?

Post and beam structural support involves the installation or repair of vertical posts and horizontal beams that form the skeletal structure of a building. This technique allows for open spaces within a home, providing a modern, airy feel while ensuring that weight is evenly distributed across the foundation. The process typically involves assessing the existing structure, determining the necessary load requirements, and installing high-strength materials like glulam beams or steel connectors to bolster the framework [Source: Litchfield, Page 221].

To maintain safety during this process, shoring posts may be utilized to temporarily support the structure while repairs are made. This method is particularly important in Seattle, where moisture and seismic activity can compromise the integrity of a home’s foundation [Source: Litchfield, Page 252].

### Common Problems This Service Solves
- **Sagging Floors**: Over time, beams may sag due to excessive load or rot, which can be corrected with proper support [Source: Litchfield, Page 256].
- **Structural Damage from Water Infiltration**: Moisture can weaken beams and posts, leading to rot. Our service helps replace or reinforce these components with pressure-treated wood or metal columns to prevent future issues [Source: Litchfield, Page 253].
- **Foundation Settling**: As homes settle, load-bearing beams may need adjustment or reinforcement to maintain integrity [Source: Litchfield, Page 252].
- **Inadequate Support for Renovations**: When undertaking home renovations, new load requirements may necessitate the installation of additional beams or posts for stability [Source: Litchfield, Page 221].
