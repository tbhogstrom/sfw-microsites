## Service Overview

### What is Foundation Beam Assessment?

Foundation beam assessment involves a comprehensive evaluation of the structural beams that support your home's foundation. It includes inspecting for signs of distress such as bowing, cracking, and uneven settlement, which may indicate underlying soil issues or insufficient steel reinforcement [Source: Renovation, Page 242]. The assessment typically requires specialized tools and techniques to determine the load-bearing capacity and overall health of the beams.

Our technicians will utilize industry-standard methods, including visual inspections and possibly structural analysis, to provide a clear picture of your foundation's condition. If necessary, we may recommend involving a structural engineer for advanced evaluations, especially for homes exhibiting severe signs of distress [Source: Renovation, Page 242].

### Why It's Important for Homeowners in Portland, Oregon

Portland's unique climate and soil composition can significantly impact the stability of your foundation. With frequent rain and potential soil saturation, your home is at risk for issues such as settling and shifting. Older homes, particularly those built without modern standards of reinforcement, may frequently experience foundation problems [Source: Renovation, Page 246]. Conducting a foundation beam assessment can help safeguard your investment and prolong the life of your home.

### Common Problems This Service Solves
- **Cracks in Beams**: Cracks that extend through the foundation can indicate serious structural issues that need immediate attention [Source: Renovation, Page 247].
- **Bowing Walls**: Walls that are bowing may signify inadequate support from the foundation beams, necessitating quick intervention [Source: Renovation, Page 242].
- **Uneven Settlement**: Homes that are settling unevenly can lead to significant structural problems, requiring a thorough assessment for remedial action [Source: Renovation, Page 246].
- **Moisture Intrusion**: Chronic wetness can weaken foundation structures, leading to rotting wood and pest infestations [Source: Renovation, Page 242].
