## Footing Repair
In Portland's rainy climate, the integrity of your home's footings is crucial. Footings serve as the base of your foundation, spreading the weight of the building evenly to prevent settling and structural shifts. Over time, water exposure can lead to erosion and damage, especially in older homes that may lack proper reinforcement<sup>1</sup>. Repairing footings involves assessing the current condition, removing damaged sections, and replacing them with new materials. This process often requires temporary shoring to stabilize the structure during repairs<sup>2</sup>. It's essential to use materials like pressure-treated lumber or preprimed metal columns to prevent future rot and decay<sup>2</sup>. If you notice signs of settling, such as uneven floors or cracks in the walls, it's time to address potential footing issues. Protect your home from further damage by seeking a professional assessment and repair plan specific to your needs.

Ensure your home's stability in the wet Pacific Northwest by scheduling a footing inspection with our team today.

## Footing Replacement
Replacing footings is a crucial step in maintaining the structural integrity of older homes in Portland, especially given the region's heavy rainfall and moisture issues. Footings serve as the foundation's base, distributing the load from the structure above and ensuring stability. Over time, water infiltration and poor initial construction can lead to footing damage, necessitating replacement. When replacing footings, it's essential to ensure the new concrete footings are properly reinforced with rebar, embedded at least 15 inches into filled cores for maximum stability<sup>3</sup>. Additionally, ensuring proper drainage and waterproofing around the new footings can protect them from future moisture-related damage<sup>4</sup>. By addressing these issues, we help prevent further settlement and structural problems. If your home's footing needs replacement, reach out to our team for a professional assessment and solution.

## Concrete Pier Repair
Concrete piers serve as essential components in distributing and stabilizing the load of your home, particularly in the Pacific Northwest where soil conditions and moisture can lead to settlement issues. Over time, environmental factors such as heavy rainfall and fluctuating temperatures can compromise the integrity of these piers, leading to cracks or shifts that threaten the structural soundness of your home. Concrete pier repair involves assessing the extent of the damage and implementing solutions that may include the use of injected epoxy, which offers a compressive strength of up to 11,000 psi, significantly higher than that of typical foundation concrete<sup>5</sup>. This method provides a reliable and long-lasting repair by bonding to both sides of the crack, effectively restoring the pier's structural role<sup>6</sup>. If you suspect your concrete piers are failing, don't wait until the problem worsens. Contact us to discuss a tailored repair plan that ensures the stability and safety of your home's foundation.

## Concrete Pier Replacement
Replacing concrete piers is a critical task for maintaining the stability of a home, especially in the Pacific Northwest where moisture and soil conditions can lead to structural challenges. Over time, concrete piers may crack or settle, compromising the support they provide to your home. When replacing a pier, it’s essential to first remove the old pier and ensure the area is adequately prepared for the new installation. This involves excavating to the appropriate depth and ensuring a stable base, often reinforced with rebar to distribute loads more effectively and minimize the risk of cracking under pressure<sup>7</sup>. 

The new concrete pier should be constructed with a mix suitable for footings and foundation walls, which typically includes a blend of cement, sand, and gravel that offers durability against the region's wet conditions<sup>8</sup>. Once poured, the concrete must cure properly to achieve maximum strength. Replacing piers is not a DIY project due to the precision and expertise required; it's best handled by professionals who can ensure the job is done safely and effectively. If your home is showing signs of foundation stress or uneven floors, consider having your concrete piers evaluated and replaced as needed to protect your home’s integrity.

## Post Footing Repair
In the Pacific Northwest, moisture can be a persistent adversary for post footings, leading to potential structural issues in your home. Post footings are crucial for transferring the load from your home's structure down to the ground, and when they fail, it often results from excessive moisture causing rot or settling issues. A common cause of wooden post failure is moisture wicking up through a concrete pad, which can rot the bottom of the post<sup>9</sup>. If your floors are sloping or you notice signs of settling, it may be due to a compromised post footing. Repairing these footings involves replacing rotted wooden posts with pressure-treated lumber or metal columns, and ensuring they are placed on adequately sized concrete pads<sup>10</sup>. These pads should be sufficiently large to distribute the load without tilting or sinking. If necessary, additional measures such as installing sump pumps can be taken to manage water issues. For durable and secure post footings, it is essential to address these problems proactively. If you suspect your post footings may be compromised, reach out to us for a thorough inspection and professional repair.

## Structural Footing Reinforcement
Reinforcing structural footings is crucial for maintaining the integrity of homes in Portland's damp climate. Over time, footings can weaken due to moisture penetration and soil movement, leading to uneven settling and structural instability. Reinforcing these footings often involves the use of steel reinforcements, such as rebar, which helps distribute loads evenly and prevents cracking caused by lateral soil pressure<sup>11</sup>. This method strengthens the entire foundation system, ensuring that pressure is managed from high-stress areas to less stressed zones, reducing the likelihood of point failure<sup>12</sup>. For homeowners, reinforcing footings not only extends the lifespan of the foundation but also enhances the overall safety and value of the property. If your home's footings show signs of distress, such as cracks or uneven floors, consider reinforcement options to prevent further damage.

## Settled Footing Repair
Settled footings can lead to uneven floors, cracked walls, and doors that won't close properly in your Portland home. The rainy climate in the Pacific Northwest can exacerbate these issues, causing soil beneath your foundation to shift and settle. Repairing settled footings requires a strategic approach to stabilize your home and prevent further movement. One effective method is underpinning, which involves extending the foundation deeper into the ground to reach more stable soil<sup>13</sup>. This process may include the use of hydraulic jacks to lift the structure back to its original level before securing it with new support elements. It is crucial to address any underlying soil issues, such as poor drainage or erosion, to ensure long-term stability<sup>14</sup>. If you notice signs of settled footings, it's essential to act quickly to protect your home's structural integrity. Contact us today to schedule an evaluation and prevent further damage.

## Foundation Support Footing Repair
Foundation support footing repair is essential for ensuring the stability and safety of your home, especially in Portland's unique climate. Footings serve as the foundation of the foundation, distributing the weight of the structure evenly to prevent uneven settlement and structural damage. In older homes, common in the Pacific Northwest, footing issues often arise from inadequate steel reinforcement or shifts in soil due to moisture<sup>15</sup>. Compacted soil is crucial for maintaining footing stability, as it minimizes the risk of undercutting and ensures that loads are transmitted straight down<sup>16</sup>. This is particularly important in areas with frequent rain, which can lead to soil erosion and compromise footing integrity. Repairing a foundation support footing involves assessing the extent of damage, stabilizing the area with shoring posts, and addressing soil compaction to restore proper load distribution. If you're noticing signs of uneven settling or cracks in your foundation, it's time to have a professional evaluation to prevent further damage and ensure your home's safety.
