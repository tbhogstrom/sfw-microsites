## Post & Column Repair
In Seattle, the challenges of rain, moisture, and aging homes make post and column repair a crucial service for maintaining structural integrity. The Pacific Northwest's damp climate can lead to rot and decay in wooden posts, weakening their load-bearing capacity. When addressing such issues, it's essential to replace rotted posts with pressure-treated lumber or preprimed metal columns to prevent future deterioration<sup>1</sup>. Additionally, ensuring the stability of posts involves using metal connectors like Simpson Strong-Tie anchors, which secure the post ends to top and sole plates, providing resistance against lateral movement and uplift<sup>2</sup>. Proper installation techniques, including plumbed posts and cross bracing, can stabilize the structure and prevent rotation during repairs. For homeowners noticing signs of sagging floors or compromised support, addressing post and column issues promptly is vital to maintaining a safe and stable home environment. Schedule an inspection with a local expert to evaluate your home's structural needs and safeguard your investment.

## Support Post Repair
Repairing support posts in your Seattle home is essential to maintaining the structural integrity of your property, especially given the region's high moisture levels and older buildings. Support posts can suffer from rot, shifting, or damage over time, leading to compromised stability. The repair process often involves removing the damaged post and replacing it with a new one. For wooden posts, it's crucial to use pressure-treated lumber to resist moisture and prevent future rot<sup>3</sup>. A 22-gauge aluminum plate can be placed beneath the new post to provide a moisture barrier and prolong the life of the post<sup>4</sup>. Once the new post is positioned correctly, metal connectors like Simpson Strong-Tie anchors can be used to secure it to the beam and foundation, ensuring stability and safety. If you're dealing with sloping floors or suspect post damage, it's time to have a professional assess and repair your support posts to keep your home safe and sound.

## Support Post Replacement
Replacing support posts in your Seattle home is crucial for maintaining structural integrity, especially given the region's wet climate and aging homes. Support posts can deteriorate due to moisture exposure, leading to rot or weakening over time. When replacing these posts, it's essential to ensure they are plumb and securely fastened to prevent movement. Start by cutting the posts slightly longer than the required height and use a sledgehammer to wedge them tightly between the floor and the beam above. Metal connectors, such as Simpson Strong-Tie A-23 anchors, should be used to secure the post ends to the top and sole plates, ensuring stability and safety<sup>5</sup>. Additionally, adding studs to both sides of the posts helps to capture and stabilize them<sup>6</sup>. This meticulous approach not only extends the lifespan of your home's structure but also offers peace of mind against potential future issues. If you're noticing signs of post degradation, now is the perfect time to replace those support posts and safeguard your home's foundation.

## Structural Post Replacement
Replacing structural posts in Seattle's homes is essential, especially considering the region's unique combination of older housing stock and moisture-rich climate. Posts are critical components that bear the weight of beams and distribute loads down to the foundation. When a post becomes compromised, it can jeopardize the entire structure's stability. Signs that a post needs replacement include visible rot, cracking, or if floors above show signs of sagging or unevenness. The replacement process involves removing the damaged post, ensuring the load is temporarily supported, and installing a new post that is plumb and securely anchored. Metal connectors like the Simpson Strong-Tie are often used to secure the post ends to the top and sole plates, ensuring stability and resistance to lateral movement<sup>7</sup>. For Seattle's wet conditions, using pressure-treated lumber or preprimed metal columns can prevent future rot and ensure long-term durability<sup>8</sup>. Homeowners noticing any signs of structural post failure should prioritize a professional assessment and replacement to maintain their home's safety and structural integrity.

## Load-Bearing Post Repair
In Seattle's damp climate, load-bearing posts are crucial to maintaining the structural integrity of your home. Over time, these posts can suffer from rot, insect damage, or shifting foundations, especially in older homes. Repairing load-bearing posts begins with identifying and addressing the root cause of the damage<sup>9</sup>. One common solution is to replace the compromised post with a new one made from pressure-treated lumber or steel, ensuring it is cut to the precise height needed to support the load properly. A metal connector, such as a Simpson Strong-Tie, is recommended to secure the post ends to the top and sole plates, preventing lateral movement<sup>10</sup>. In cases where the post is part of a larger structural system, it's vital to ensure continuous support down to the foundation. This might involve adding blocking or a concrete footing to distribute the load effectively. If you suspect issues with your load-bearing posts, don't wait for further damage—addressing the problem early can prevent more extensive repairs down the line.

## Wood Post Rot Repair
Wood post rot is a common issue for Seattle homeowners, especially with our wet Pacific Northwest climate contributing to moisture accumulation. When wood posts are exposed to water over prolonged periods, they can absorb moisture, leading to rot. This not only compromises the structural integrity of the post but also the safety of the entire structure it supports. To effectively repair rot in wood posts, it's crucial to first identify the extent of the damage. Start by removing the rotted sections back to solid wood, ensuring that all compromised material is eliminated. Using a high-strength epoxy can effectively seal and reinforce the remaining wood, prolonging its lifespan<sup>11</sup>. However, if the rot is extensive, replacing the entire post might be necessary to ensure safety and durability<sup>12</sup>. For the best results, consider using pressure-treated wood or applying a water-resistant sealant to the new or repaired posts to prevent future damage. ## Steel Support Post Installation
Steel support posts are an ideal choice for Seattle homeowners looking to reinforce their home's structural integrity, especially in areas prone to moisture and decay. These posts provide exceptional strength and stability, effectively distributing loads to the foundation. Steel's resistance to rot and insect damage makes it particularly suitable for wet climates like the Pacific Northwest<sup>13</sup>. ## Adjustable Post Installation
Adjustable post installation is an effective solution for addressing uneven or shifting foundations in Seattle's older homes, often challenged by the city's wet climate. These posts, commonly known as screw jacks, provide an adaptable support system that can be fine-tuned to stabilize various structural elements, such as beams and floors. By using adjustable posts, you can maintain a level structure and prevent further damage caused by settling or moisture-induced ground movement. 

The installation process involves placing the screw jacks under the load-bearing points and adjusting them to the required height. It’s important to ensure the posts are plumbed accurately to avoid any lateral movement. Steel caps are typically nailed to the tops of the shoring posts to keep them securely in place and prevent rotation, ensuring the beam remains stable<sup>15</sup>. With the posts precisely adjusted, the structure is supported until any necessary repairs can be made, such as replacing rotted wood or reinforcing existing beams<sup>16</sup>. 

Whether you're dealing with a basement renovation or a sagging floor, installing adjustable posts is a proactive step in maintaining the structural integrity of your home. If you suspect your foundation needs leveling, consider adjustable post installation to safeguard your property.
