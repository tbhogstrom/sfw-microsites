## Service Overview

### What is Restoration of Exposed Beams?

Restoration of exposed beams involves a comprehensive process that includes assessment, repair, and finishing of beams to restore their original beauty and functionality. It typically involves the following steps: 

1. **Assessment**: Identify any structural damage, rot, or pest infestations.
2. **Repair**: Depending on the findings, this may involve reinforcing, replacing, or treating the beams.
3. **Finishing**: This may include sanding, staining, or sealing to protect the wood and enhance its appearance.

Technical details include using proper shoring techniques with 4x4 posts and jacks to safely support beams during restoration. According to experts, ensuring that both ends of the beam are properly supported is crucial for maintaining structural integrity [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 221].

### Common Problems This Service Solves
- **Structural Damage**: Over time, beams may sag or crack under pressure, requiring professional reinforcement [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 255].
- **Moisture Damage**: Exposure to Seattle's wet climate can lead to rot, which can be effectively treated and repaired through restoration [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 75].
