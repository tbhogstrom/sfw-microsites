## Service Overview

### What is Foundation and Framing Inspection?

Foundation and framing inspection involves a thorough evaluation of your home's structural components, including the foundation, framing, and associated elements. This process includes checking for cracks in the foundation, signs of moisture intrusion, and assessing the overall stability of the structure. Our inspectors utilize advanced techniques to identify critical issues such as horizontal soil pressures causing foundation rotation and the presence of rot in wooden framing members. According to industry standards, serious cracks are defined as gaps that are at least 1/4 inch wide and indicate underlying structural problems [Source: Litchfield, Page 27].

### Why It's Important for Homeowners in Portland, Oregon

Portland's unique climate, characterized by wet winters and dry summers, creates conditions that can lead to foundation and framing issues. Homeowners may face challenges such as shifting soil, increased moisture levels, and pests that can compromise the integrity of their homes. Additionally, local building codes require compliance with specific standards to ensure safety and durability. Regular inspections help homeowners identify issues early, preventing extensive damage and ensuring compliance with local regulations.

### Common Problems This Service Solves
- **Cracks in Foundation**: Vertical or horizontal cracks can indicate significant structural issues, often caused by differential settlement or hydrostatic pressure [Source: Litchfield, Page 242].
- **Moisture Intrusion**: Water seeping through foundations can lead to rot and mold, which are health hazards and can compromise structural integrity [Source: Litchfield, Page 246].
- **Rotten Framing**: Wooden framing exposed to moisture can deteriorate, requiring extensive repairs if not addressed promptly [Source: Litchfield, Page 555].
- **Uneven Settling**: A foundation showing signs of bowing or uneven settling requires immediate attention to prevent further damage [Source: Litchfield, Page 242].
