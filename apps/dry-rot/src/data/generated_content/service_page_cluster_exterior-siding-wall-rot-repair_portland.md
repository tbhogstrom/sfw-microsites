## Rotten Siding Repair Service
Rotten siding is a common issue for Portland homeowners, especially given the region's persistent rain and moisture. To assess the extent of the damage, start by gently probing the exterior siding with a pocketknife or awl. If the tool easily penetrates the siding, it's a clear indication of underlying rot<sup>1</sup>. When dealing with rotten siding, it's crucial to understand that not all damaged panels need replacement. Some wooden and fiber-cement sidings can be repaired in place, using techniques such as filling gouges and splits or patching smaller rot areas<sup>2</sup>. However, if the rot is extensive, replacing the damaged sections might be necessary to prevent further deterioration and maintain the structural integrity of your home. To ensure your home remains protected from the elements, it's essential to address any signs of rot promptly. If you suspect your siding has been compromised, take the first step by scheduling an inspection to determine the best repair approach for your home.

## Exterior Siding Rot Repair
Dealing with rot on your home’s exterior siding is a common issue for Portland homeowners, especially given the wet climate of the Pacific Northwest. Moisture is the main culprit behind siding rot, often exacerbated by improper maintenance and environmental factors like foliage too close to the house or poorly directed sprinklers<sup>3</sup>. Regular inspection of your home’s siding is essential. Look for discoloration, warping, or areas where the siding feels soft to the touch, as these are signs that rot may have set in<sup>4</sup>. Repairing rot involves removing the affected sections and replacing them with new, weather-resistant materials, ensuring that any underlying moisture issues are addressed to prevent recurrence. Our team at SFW Construction specializes in efficient and effective siding rot repair, using quality materials to restore your home’s integrity and appearance. ## Dry Rot Siding Replacement
Dry rot is a common issue in Portland's damp climate, often necessitating siding replacement to restore your home's integrity. When dealing with dry rot, it's crucial to identify and replace affected sections promptly to prevent further damage. The removal process involves careful extraction of the compromised siding, ensuring that the underlying structure remains intact. This means determining how the siding is attached and removing it without damaging the weather-resistive barrier, such as housewrap, which protects your home from moisture<sup>5</sup>. While some minor repairs can be done without full replacement, dry rot usually requires replacing the affected wood entirely to ensure long-term stability and appearance<sup>6</sup>. Choosing the right siding material is essential for longevity and performance; options like fiber cement or treated wood are particularly well-suited for the Pacific Northwest's wet conditions. If you suspect dry rot in your siding, schedule an inspection today to protect your home from further damage.

## Lap Siding Rot Repair
Lap siding is a common choice for Pacific Northwest homes due to its classic appearance and durability. However, the region's persistent rain and moisture can lead to rot, particularly if the siding is not regularly maintained. To repair rot in lap siding effectively, start by identifying the extent of the damage. Use a pocketknife or awl to gently probe the siding-rot areas; if the tool easily penetrates, the siding is compromised<sup>7</sup>. Removing the damaged siding is crucial; use a flat bar or shingle ripper to carefully lift the affected sections without harming adjacent boards<sup>8</sup>. Once removed, inspect the underlying sheathing for any signs of rot, and address those areas as needed. Ensure new siding sections are treated with water repellent before installation to minimize future issues. Secure the new pieces with rust-resistant nails, and consider repainting or staining the repaired area to ensure a seamless look and added protection. Don't let rot compromise your home's exterior; act promptly to maintain the integrity and appearance of your lap siding.

Take advantage of our professional lap siding repair services to address rot before it spreads further.

## Cedar Shake Rot Replacement
Cedar shakes are a popular siding choice in Portland for their natural beauty and durability. However, the rainy Pacific Northwest climate can accelerate rot, especially if they aren't properly maintained. Rot often begins at the ends of the shakes, where moisture tends to accumulate<sup>9</sup>. To effectively replace rotted cedar shakes, it's crucial to cut back to undamaged wood. This process involves removing the compromised sections and installing new shakes with proper spacing to ensure adequate air circulation, which helps prevent future moisture buildup<sup>10</sup>. It's also advisable to use cedar that is naturally resistant to decay, such as western red cedar, which has inherent insecticidal and fungicidal properties<sup>10</sup>. When replacing cedar shakes, ensure that the new materials are treated with a weather-resistant finish to further protect against the elements. If you're noticing signs of rot in your cedar shakes, it's time to have them assessed for replacement to maintain the integrity and appearance of your home.

## Exterior Wall Sheathing Rot Repair
Exterior wall sheathing is a crucial component of your home's structure, protecting it from the elements while providing stability. In the damp climate of Portland, Oregon, this sheathing is particularly susceptible to rot due to persistent moisture exposure. Signs of rot in wall sheathing include bulging walls, peeling paint, or a musty odor. Once rot sets in, it compromises the structural integrity of your home, leading to further damage if not addressed promptly<sup>11</sup>. Repairing rotted sheathing involves removing the affected material and replacing it with new, treated sheathing to prevent future decay. It's essential to ensure that the new sheathing is properly sealed and that any sources of moisture are addressed to avoid recurrence<sup>12</sup>. This might include repairing gutters, ensuring proper grading around the house, or installing a rain-screen wall system to enhance ventilation. Don't let rot undermine your home's structural health; contact us today to assess and repair any wall sheathing issues before they escalate.

## Corner Board Rot Repair
Corner boards are crucial in protecting the edges of your home from the Pacific Northwest's relentless rain and moisture, but they can fall victim to rot over time. Rot often starts at the bottom ends of corner boards, where water collects and seeps in, leading to damage that can compromise the structural integrity of your home. The repair process typically involves removing the rotted section and replacing it with a new piece of wood. A straightforward way to accomplish this is by making a clean 90º cut into the sound wood using a circular saw, then attaching a new section with high-strength epoxy or construction adhesive<sup>13</sup>. This method ensures a lasting repair that withstands seasonal movement and moisture. It's also essential to seal and paint the new wood to protect it from future water damage<sup>14</sup>. Don't let rot compromise the corners of your home; consider replacing those damaged corner boards to maintain your home's integrity and appearance.
