## Rotted Window Sill Replacement
Replacing a rotted window sill is essential for maintaining the integrity of your home, especially in Seattle's damp climate. When moisture infiltrates the sill, it can lead to decay and further damage to the surrounding structure. The first step in replacing a rotted sill is to remove the old unit entirely, including any compromised wood around the rough opening<sup>1</sup>. This ensures a fresh start with a sound foundation. Next, using pressure-treated lumber for the new sill is advisable, as it offers enhanced resistance to moisture, although it may need some time to hold paint effectively<sup>2</sup>. Proper installation includes creating a slight slope on the top surface to shed water effectively and prevent future rot. Additionally, applying a self-adhesive flashing along the sill and integrating a drip edge can help direct water away from the window frame. If you're dealing with a rotted window sill, contact us to ensure a long-lasting repair that stands up to the Pacific Northwest weather.

## Window Frame Rot Repair
Window frame rot is a common issue for Seattle homeowners, particularly given the region's persistent rain and humidity. Moisture can infiltrate window frames, causing wood to decay over time. Repairing rot effectively involves removing the damaged sections and replacing them with new wood. In some cases, if the rot is limited, a two-part epoxy putty can be used to rebuild the affected areas<sup>3</sup>. It’s essential to identify how water is entering the frame and address this issue to prevent future damage. This may involve adjusting the window installation or sealing methods to enhance water resistance<sup>4</sup>. Proper sealing and flashing during repairs can safeguard against future moisture intrusion. If you suspect window frame rot, it's crucial to act quickly to prevent further structural damage and maintain energy efficiency. Reach out to us today to assess and repair your window frames.

## Window Sill Rot Repair
Window sill rot is a common issue for Seattle homeowners due to the region's persistent rain and moisture. Unaddressed sill rot can lead to more severe structural problems, as water can seep into the framing and cause further decay. The first step in repairing a rotted window sill is to carefully remove all decayed wood. Once the damaged areas are cleared, it's crucial to install a watertight barrier. Utilizing self-adhesive flashing tape can create a protective pan that directs water away from the sill, preventing future rot<sup>5</sup>. After securing the watertight barrier, a new sill can be crafted from durable materials like Douglas fir, which is known for its natural water resistance<sup>6</sup>. Finally, the new sill should be primed and painted with a high-quality exterior paint to add an additional layer of protection against the elements. If you're noticing signs of rot in your window sills, don't wait for the problem to get worse—schedule a professional assessment today.

## Bay Window Rot Repair
Bay windows are a charming feature of many Seattle homes, but they are also particularly susceptible to rot due to their exposed structure and multiple joints. The Pacific Northwest's wet climate can exacerbate this issue, leading to compromised window integrity. When addressing rot in bay windows, it is essential to first identify the source of moisture infiltration. This might involve checking the condition of flashing and seals, which are critical in preventing water from reaching the wooden components<sup>7</sup>. Once the rot is identified, the affected sections must be carefully removed. Depending on the extent of the damage, it may involve replacing entire sections of the frame or using high-quality two-part epoxy putties for minor repairs<sup>8</sup>. After repairs, it is crucial to restore the weatherproofing by installing new flashing and applying a protective sealant. This not only extends the life of your bay window but also enhances the energy efficiency of your home. To ensure your bay windows are both beautiful and functional, consider having a professional inspection to assess potential rot and suggest the best repair options.

## Sliding Door Frame Rot Repair
Sliding door frames are particularly vulnerable to rot, especially in the damp climate of Seattle, where moisture can easily seep into wood structures. Over time, this exposure can lead to decay, compromising the door’s structural integrity. Repairing a rotted sliding door frame begins with a thorough inspection to identify all areas affected by rot. Once identified, you'll need to remove the decayed wood carefully and replace it with treated wood that resists moisture. It's essential to ensure that the replacement wood fits snugly to prevent further water intrusion. Additionally, weatherstripping and sealing are crucial to creating a barrier against moisture, which not only helps in preserving the new frame but also increases energy efficiency by preventing drafts<sup>9</sup>. To prevent future rot, consider installing a roof or porch overhang to shield the door from rain<sup>10</sup>. If your sliding door frame shows signs of rot, it's essential to address the problem promptly to maintain the safety and functionality of your door. ## French Door Rot Repair
French doors are a popular choice for Seattle homeowners due to their elegant appeal and ability to let natural light flood into your home. However, the Pacific Northwest's heavy rainfall can lead to rot, particularly if the doors aren't adequately protected by an overhang or if the seals are compromised. The first signs of rot often appear where moisture is most likely to accumulate, such as at the bottom of the door or along the jambs. To repair rot effectively, it’s crucial to remove all damaged wood and replace it with new, treated timber. Applying a flexible, self-adhesive flashing tape over the top flange of the head casing can help prevent future water infiltration<sup>11</sup>. Additionally, ensuring that the doors are properly sealed with durable weatherstripping can significantly extend their lifespan<sup>12</sup>. If you notice signs of rot in your French doors, it's essential to address the issue promptly to maintain the structural integrity and aesthetic appeal of your home. Contact us today to schedule a professional assessment and repair service for your French doors.

## Garage Door Frame Rot Repair
Garage door frames are especially susceptible to rot due to their constant exposure to the elements in the Pacific Northwest climate. Moisture from rain can seep into the frame, leading to wood rot over time. If left unchecked, this can compromise the structural integrity of your garage door frame, making it difficult for the door to operate smoothly or even causing it to become misaligned. ## Rotted Window Trim & Sashes
Rotted window trim and sashes are a common issue in Seattle due to the region's damp climate. Older homes, many of which have windows made from rot-resistant fir or redwood, often face deterioration as these materials age and weather exposure increases<sup>15</sup>. Proper maintenance is crucial to extend the life of these components. When rot is present, removing the damaged sections and replacing them with new wood is essential. For minor rot, epoxy putties can be used to rebuild decayed parts, ensuring the window remains functional and aesthetically pleasing<sup>16</sup>. Refurbishing involves cleaning, sanding, and priming bare wood, followed by applying a high-quality finish to protect against moisture. It's also important to inspect and repair any caulking or weatherstripping to prevent further moisture ingress. If your window trim or sashes are showing signs of rot, it's time to take action before the damage worsens. Consider reaching out to a local expert to assess and address your repair needs.

## Rotted Door or Window Jambs
Rotted door or window jambs are a common issue in the damp climate of Seattle, especially for older homes. Jambs are essential structural components that support doors and windows, and their integrity is crucial for maintaining a secure and weatherproof home. Rot often occurs when moisture infiltrates wood, creating an environment for fungi to thrive. This is particularly problematic in the Pacific Northwest, where rain and damp conditions are prevalent. To address rotted jambs, it’s important to first remove any decayed wood and identify the extent of the damage. Replacement of the affected section may be necessary, ensuring that the new wood is properly sealed and protected against future moisture exposure. Using materials like self-adhesive flashing tape can help create a watertight seal around the repaired areas<sup>17</sup>. Additionally, ensuring a proper fit with a consistent reveal between the door or window and the jamb is essential to prevent future issues<sup>18</sup>. If you suspect your door or window jambs might be rotting, schedule an inspection to assess the damage and discuss repair options with our experts.
