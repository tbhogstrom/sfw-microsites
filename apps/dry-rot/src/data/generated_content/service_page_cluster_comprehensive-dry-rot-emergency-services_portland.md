## Comprehensive Dry Rot Solutions
Dry rot is a common issue for Portland homeowners, especially given the region's damp climate. It is essential to address dry rot promptly to prevent structural damage. Our comprehensive dry rot solutions are designed to tackle this problem efficiently and effectively. We begin by conducting a thorough assessment to identify the extent of the damage, ensuring we target all affected areas. Our team uses advanced techniques, including high-strength epoxy repairs, which are durable and ensure a long-lasting fix<sup>1</sup>. Cutting back to undamaged wood at a 45-degree angle, known as a scarf joint, is a common method we use to remove compromised sections before restoring structural integrity<sup>2</sup>. This approach not only repairs the damage but also prevents future rot from setting in. By using moisture-resistant materials and ensuring proper ventilation, we help safeguard your home against recurring issues. If you're facing dry rot problems, let us provide a tailored solution to protect your home from further damage.

## Rotten Window Sill Repair
Rotten window sills are a common issue for many Portland homes, often resulting from the relentless Pacific Northwest rain. These sills, if not properly maintained, can lead to further structural damage by allowing water to seep into the walls. The key to repairing a rotten window sill is to first remove all the damaged wood. This involves using a chisel or saw to cut away the rot and ensure the remaining wood is healthy. Once the rot is removed, it's crucial to install a new sill with a slight pitch to effectively shed water away from the house<sup>5</sup>. Additionally, using a durable material like pressure-treated wood can extend the life of the replacement sill, but be aware it may not hold paint well until it has aged<sup>6</sup>. To ensure a watertight seal, apply flexible, self-adhesive flashing tape along the sill and use high-quality caulking around the edges. This method not only restores the visual appeal of your window but also reinforces its integrity against moisture. If you suspect your window sill is compromised, now is the time to inspect and repair it to prevent further damage.

## Wood Rot Damage Repair
Wood rot damage can compromise the structural integrity of your home, especially in the damp Pacific Northwest climate. It's essential to address wood rot promptly to prevent further damage. The first step in repairing wood rot damage is to assess the extent of the decay and determine the root cause, such as moisture infiltration. This often involves cutting back to solid wood, but in some cases, soft wood can be reinforced using specialized products like epoxy fillers<sup>9</sup>. Begin by removing the decayed wood using a chisel and vacuuming up debris. Allow the area to dry thoroughly, as moisture can hinder the effectiveness of repairs. Once dry, apply a wood hardener to stabilize the area and prevent further decay. Follow with a two-part epoxy filler to fill any gaps, ensuring a smooth finish<sup>10</sup>. This method will not only restore the aesthetic appeal but also lend structural support to the affected area. If you're unsure about tackling wood rot damage on your own, consult a professional to ensure a long-lasting repair.

Take action today by inspecting your home for signs of wood rot and addressing any issues before they worsen.

## Water Damage Wood Repair
Water damage is a common issue in Portland homes, especially with the region's notorious rainfall. When wood is exposed to moisture, it can swell, warp, and ultimately succumb to rot if not addressed promptly<sup>11</sup>. Repairing water-damaged wood involves several key steps to ensure the longevity and integrity of the repair. First, it's crucial to thoroughly dry the affected area, as moisture trapped in the wood can continue to cause damage over time. Once dry, remove any rotted sections of the wood. This may involve cutting back to solid wood, which can then be reinforced or replaced as needed<sup>12</sup>. Applying a wood hardener can stabilize the remaining material before filling gaps with a two-part epoxy filler. This approach not only restores the wood's structural integrity but also prepares it for a durable finish. Avoiding future damage requires addressing the root cause of the water exposure, such as fixing leaks or improving drainage around the home. If you're dealing with water-damaged wood, our skilled team is ready to evaluate and restore your wood surfaces to their former glory.

## Exterior Wood Restoration
Restoring exterior wood on your Portland home is crucial to maintain its structural integrity and aesthetic appeal, especially given the Pacific Northwest's notorious wet climate. Wood exposed to moisture is susceptible to rot, particularly if not properly sealed. The first step in exterior wood restoration is assessing the condition of the wood. Use a pocketknife or an awl to test for rot or insect damage, ensuring that any salvaged wood is structurally sound<sup>13</sup>. Once the wood is confirmed to be sound, it should be protected from further moisture absorption. Applying a penetrating sealer can form a hard shell on the wood, offering protection against the elements while maintaining the wood's natural appearance<sup>14</sup>. This will help prevent future damage and prolong the life of your wood surfaces. Don't wait until it's too late; take proactive steps to restore and protect your home's exterior wood to ensure it lasts for years to come.
