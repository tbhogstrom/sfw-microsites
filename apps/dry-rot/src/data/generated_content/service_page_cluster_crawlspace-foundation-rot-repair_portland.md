## Crawlspace Beam Rot Repair
Crawlspace beam rot is a common issue in Portland homes, where the damp climate can accelerate wood rot in crawlspaces. Moisture is the primary culprit, as fungi responsible for wood rot thrive in environments with a moisture content above 28%<sup>1</sup>. Before repairing a rotted beam, it's crucial to eliminate excess moisture by improving drainage, maintaining gutters, and ensuring proper ventilation in the crawlspace<sup>2</sup>. Once moisture control measures are in place, the next step is to replace the rotten beam with a new one. This involves supporting the structure with shoring posts and jacks to safely remove the damaged beam. The replacement beam should be pressure-treated to resist future rot. Properly installing the new beam and ensuring it is well-supported will prevent the recurrence of structural issues. If you're dealing with a rotted crawlspace beam, call us to ensure a durable and effective repair.

## Crawlspace Joist Rot Repair
Crawlspace joist rot is a common issue for Portland homeowners, exacerbated by the moist climate and typical construction practices. Joists in older homes are especially vulnerable to rot due to prolonged exposure to damp conditions, often caused by insufficient ventilation or moisture barriers in crawlspaces<sup>3</sup>. When moisture levels rise above 28%, wood becomes susceptible to fungi that cause rot<sup>4</sup>. To effectively repair rotted joists, it's crucial to first address the moisture problem by improving drainage, installing a vapor barrier, and ensuring proper ventilation. Once the environmental conditions are under control, replace the damaged joists with pressure-treated lumber, which is more resistant to rot. Installing a continuous moisture barrier and sealing gaps can help prevent future rot. If you're experiencing sagging floors or other symptoms of joist rot, it's essential to address the issue promptly to maintain your home's structural integrity. Act now to protect your investment by ensuring your crawlspace is dry and your joists are free from rot.

## Mud Sill Rot Repair
Mud sills are critical components of your home's foundation, providing a stable base for the structure above. Unfortunately, the damp climate in Portland makes them susceptible to rot, especially if moisture is allowed to accumulate over time<sup>5</sup>. Identifying and repairing rotted mud sills promptly is crucial to maintaining your home's structural integrity. The first step in addressing mud sill rot is to identify the extent of the damage. Using a probe, such as a screwdriver or awl, check for spongy wood or areas where the wood crumbles easily. Once identified, the rotted sections must be removed and replaced with treated lumber to resist future rot<sup>6</sup>. Proper sealing and ventilation of crawlspaces can further prevent moisture build-up and subsequent rot. If you're noticing signs of rot in your mud sills, it's vital to act quickly to prevent further damage. Ensure your home's foundation remains strong by scheduling a professional inspection and repair today.

## Foundation Plate Rot Repair
Foundation plate rot can compromise the structural integrity of your home, especially in the damp climate of Portland, Oregon. This component, sitting atop the foundation wall, is crucial for distributing the weight of the house. When rot sets in, often due to persistent moisture or poor drainage, it weakens the plate and can lead to significant structural issues<sup>7</sup>. Repairing a rotted foundation plate involves carefully assessing the extent of the damage and removing the compromised wood. It's essential to replace it with pressure-treated lumber to resist future moisture intrusion<sup>8</sup>. Proper installation ensures the new plate is securely anchored to the foundation, often using galvanized steel for added durability. An essential part of this process is improving water drainage around the home to prevent moisture from accumulating. This can involve grading the landscape away from the foundation or installing drainage systems. Ensuring the stability of your foundation plate is critical; if you suspect rot, act quickly to prevent further damage. Contact us to schedule a thorough inspection and protect your home's foundation today.

## Moisture-Damaged Framing Repair
Moisture-damaged framing is a common issue for Portland homeowners, particularly due to the region's high rainfall. When moisture infiltrates your home's framing, it can lead to wood rot, mold growth, and structural weakening. Identifying the source of moisture is crucial, as it often enters through inadequately flashed roofs, blocked gutters, or unsealed windows and doors<sup>9</sup>. Once moisture is detected, it's important to remove and replace any rotted wood to prevent further damage. This process involves cutting out the deteriorated sections and replacing them with new, treated lumber to ensure longevity<sup>10</sup>. It's also vital to address the moisture source to prevent recurrence. As you consider repairs, consult with professionals who understand the unique challenges of Pacific Northwest homes. To protect your home from future issues, start by inspecting your framing for signs of moisture damage today.

## Subfloor Rot Removal
Subfloor rot is a common problem for homeowners in the Pacific Northwest, where moisture and dampness can easily penetrate wooden structures. Over time, a chronically damp subfloor can swell, split, and eventually rot, compromising the structural integrity of your home<sup>11</sup>. The first step in dealing with subfloor rot is to identify and eliminate the source of moisture. This might involve improving ventilation in crawlspaces or installing a vapor barrier to limit moisture ingress<sup>12</sup>. Once the moisture issue is resolved, the damaged subfloor needs to be carefully removed. This typically involves cutting out the affected sections and replacing them with new, dry materials that match the existing subfloor's thickness and type to ensure consistency and stability. It's crucial to use a moisture meter during this process to confirm that the new materials are at the appropriate moisture content before installation<sup>11</sup>. Addressing subfloor rot promptly can prevent further damage and safeguard your home’s structure. If you suspect your subfloor is compromised, it's time to schedule a professional inspection to assess the damage and explore your repair options.

## Rotted Sill Plate Removal
Rotted sill plate removal is a crucial repair for maintaining the structural integrity of your Portland home, especially given the damp conditions of the Pacific Northwest. Sill plates, which sit directly on the foundation, are susceptible to rot due to persistent moisture exposure. When these wooden components become compromised, they can lead to serious issues in your home’s framework. The process begins with a thorough inspection to assess the extent of the rot. If the sill plate is spongy or visibly deteriorated, it needs to be removed entirely. After removing the damaged section, the area must be cleaned and dried thoroughly to ensure a solid foundation for the new plate. Using pressure-treated lumber is recommended for replacement, as it is more resistant to moisture and decay<sup>13</sup>. Additionally, installing a moisture barrier between the foundation and the new sill plate can provide extra protection against future rot<sup>14</sup>. If your sill plates are showing signs of damage, take action now to replace them and protect your home's structural health.
