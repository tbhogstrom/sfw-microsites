## Service Overview

### What is Kitchen Plumbing Leak Repair?

Kitchen plumbing leak repair involves identifying, diagnosing, and fixing leaks in various kitchen plumbing fixtures and pipes. This service includes inspecting faucets, supply lines, drain pipes, and connections for signs of leaks, corrosion, or damage. Our qualified technicians may use specialized tools and methods to repair or replace faulty components, ensuring a watertight seal and optimal functionality [Source: Litchfield, Page 471].

Common tasks in this process may include:
- Assessing the condition of pipes and fittings, particularly under sinks and behind cabinets.
- Replacing worn-out washers, gaskets, or seals that may lead to leaks.
- Utilizing compression fittings or dielectric unions to ensure proper connections between dissimilar metals to prevent galvanic corrosion [Source: Litchfield, Page 29; Page 6].
- Ensuring that all repairs comply with local plumbing codes to maintain safety and functionality.

### Common Problems This Service Solves
- **Worn Out Washers and Seals**: Often, a simple replacement of a $2 wax gasket or worn washer can prevent extensive water damage [Source: Litchfield, Page 393].
- **Corroded Pipes**: Identifying and replacing corroded pipes, especially in older homes, can prevent leaks that compromise the structural integrity of your kitchen [Source: Litchfield, Page 26].
- **Faulty Connections and Fittings**: Using compression fittings and dielectric unions can resolve issues caused by dissimilar metals and prevent future leaks [Source: Litchfield, Page 29].
