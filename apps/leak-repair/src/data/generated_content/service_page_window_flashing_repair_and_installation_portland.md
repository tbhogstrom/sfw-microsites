## Service Overview

### What is Window Flashing Repair and Installation?

Window flashing repair and installation involves the application of specialized materials around windows to create a waterproof barrier. This process includes the installation of flexible flashing, rigid sill pans, and metal cap flashings, which are essential for redirecting water away from window frames and preventing moisture penetration. According to industry standards, flashing should extend past the window's nailing flanges and overlap with the building's exterior cladding to ensure optimal water diversion [Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 141].

### Why It's Important for Homeowners in Portland, Oregon

Portland is known for its wet climate, with frequent rainfall that can lead to water-related issues if not managed properly. Installing and maintaining proper window flashing is crucial to avoid rotting frames, mold growth, and costly repairs. Local building codes often require adherence to specific flashing installation methods to ensure homes can withstand the Pacific Northwest's weather [Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 140].

### Common Problems This Service Solves
- **Water Infiltration**: Improperly installed or degraded flashing can lead to leaks. Proper flashing directs water away from the window, preventing infiltration [Source: Charlie Wing - The Visual Handbook of Building and Remodeling, Page 236].
- **Mold Growth**: Moisture accumulation around windows can lead to mold, posing health risks. Effective flashing prevents moisture buildup [Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 166].
- **Structural Damage**: Water that seeps into the walls can cause rot and compromise structural integrity. Quality flashing protects against such damage [Source: Michael Litchfield - Renovation, Page 148].
- **Energy Loss**: Poorly sealed windows can lead to energy inefficiency. Properly installed flashing contributes to better insulation and reduced energy costs [Source: Charlie Wing - The Visual Handbook of Building and Remodeling, Page 300].
