## Fascia Board Repair
Fascia boards are crucial components of your home's roofline, protecting the edge of the roof and supporting the gutter system. In Seattle's damp climate, these boards often face issues like rot due to persistent moisture. Effective fascia board repair involves removing the damaged section and replacing it with a new piece, ensuring a seamless fit to maintain structural integrity. When dealing with rot, it’s vital to cut back to sound wood, typically at a 45-degree angle to prevent further decay<sup>1</sup>. Using high-strength epoxy to create a butt joint can provide a durable solution, prolonging the life of the repair and matching the longevity of the original wood<sup>2</sup>. It's essential to address these repairs promptly to prevent water from seeping into the roof structure, potentially leading to more significant damage. If your fascia boards are showing signs of wear, schedule a repair assessment to keep your home protected against Seattle's relentless rain.

## Soffit Trim Repair
Soffit trim plays a crucial role in maintaining the ventilation and structural integrity of your roofline. In Seattle, with its frequent rainfall, soffit trim can be particularly vulnerable to moisture damage, leading to rot and decay over time. Repairing soffit trim involves evaluating the extent of the damage and either repairing or replacing the affected sections. According to best practices, trim repair should begin from the bottom and work upwards to ensure each element sheds water properly over the previous one<sup>3</sup>. This helps prevent future water infiltration and extends the life of your repairs. When addressing soffit trim issues, it's essential to use materials that can endure the Pacific Northwest's wet climate. Incorporating PVC trim, which is resistant to moisture and does not require frequent painting, can be a smart choice for a longer-lasting solution<sup>4</sup>. If your soffit trim is showing signs of damage, don't wait for the problem to worsen. Contact us today to evaluate and repair your soffit trim, ensuring your home remains protected against the elements.

## Rotted Fascia Board Replacement
Replacing a rotted fascia board is crucial for maintaining the structural integrity and aesthetic appeal of your home in the damp climate of Seattle. Fascia boards are particularly susceptible to rot due to their exposure to rain and moisture. When replacing these boards, it's essential to cut back to undamaged wood at a 45° angle, creating a secure scarf joint for the new section to attach seamlessly<sup>5</sup>. For a more lasting repair, consider using a butt joint with high-strength epoxy, which can endure harsh weather conditions and prevent future rot<sup>6</sup>. Choosing a replacement board that matches the original in color and grain ensures a cohesive look with the rest of your home. Once the replacement is complete, seal and paint the new fascia to protect it from moisture. If you notice any signs of wood rot on your fascia, it's time to schedule a professional inspection and replacement to protect your home from further damage.

## Rafter Tail Trim Repair
Rafter tail trim repair is essential for Seattle homes, especially given the Pacific Northwest's wet climate that can accelerate wood rot. Rafter tails, which extend beyond the roofline, are particularly vulnerable to moisture damage, leading to potential structural issues if not addressed promptly. The repair process involves carefully removing the damaged wood and replacing it with a new section that matches the original design. Using a circular saw and speed square, make a precise 90-degree cut into the sound wood to prepare for the new trim installation.<sup>7</sup> To ensure a long-lasting repair, high-strength marine epoxy can be used to bond the new rafter tail securely in place, offering resistance to the elements and preventing future deterioration.<sup>8</sup> Proper alignment and installation of the replacement piece are crucial to maintaining the home's aesthetic and structural integrity. If you notice any signs of rafter tail damage, such as sagging or visible rot, it's time to act quickly to avoid further complications.

## Roofline Trim Repair
Maintaining the roofline trim on your Seattle home is crucial for both its aesthetic appeal and structural integrity. Roofline trim, including fascia and soffit boards, acts as a first line of defense against the Pacific Northwest's relentless rain and moisture. ## Eave Trim Repair
Eave trim plays a crucial role in protecting your home from water damage and ensuring proper ventilation. In Seattle's wet climate, eaves are constantly exposed to moisture, making them susceptible to rot and decay. Repairing eave trim involves removing any damaged sections and replacing them with new materials. ## Gable Trim Repair
Gable trim is a crucial component of your home's exterior, especially in the rainy Pacific Northwest. It serves not only as a decorative element but also provides essential protection against water infiltration at the roof and wall intersections. Ensuring your gable trim is in good condition is vital to prevent moisture-related issues that can lead to costly repairs. When repairing gable trim, it's important to check for any signs of rot or damage, particularly at the J-channel where siding meets the roofline. If you're replacing the trim, ensure you create a pattern that matches the slope of the gable to maintain a seamless appearance<sup>13</sup>. Additionally, using quality materials like aluminum nails can help avoid future problems such as rust or separation, ensuring the longevity of the repair<sup>14</sup>. For homeowners in Seattle, who often deal with persistent rain, maintaining the integrity of gable trim is crucial. If you notice any damage or deterioration in your gable trim, it's time to schedule a professional assessment and repair to protect your home from the elements.

## Barge Board Repair
Barge boards, an essential component of your roofline, often serve both a decorative and protective role by shielding the roof's edges from water damage. In Seattle's rainy climate, these boards can become susceptible to moisture infiltration, leading to rot or decay. Repairing barge boards involves carefully assessing the extent of the damage and cutting back to sound wood. This is achieved with a simple 90º cut using a circular saw and a speed square<sup>15</sup>. Once the damaged portion is removed, a new section of trim, ideally made from the same species of wood as the original, is attached using high-strength marine epoxy and a plastic biscuit for a seamless and durable repair<sup>16</sup>. Ensuring a proper repair not only restores the aesthetic appeal of your home but also fortifies the roofline against future weather challenges. If you suspect your barge boards are compromised, act swiftly to avoid more extensive damage and increased repair costs by scheduling a professional assessment today.

## Frieze Board Repair
Frieze boards play a crucial role in protecting your home from moisture, especially in the Pacific Northwest where rain is frequent. Positioned at the intersection of the wall and the roof, frieze boards act as a barrier against water intrusion and can also conceal eave vents<sup>17</sup>. Over time, exposure to the elements can lead to rot and deterioration, compromising their effectiveness. When repairing frieze boards, it's essential to cut back to sound wood. A simple 90° cut with a circular saw and a speed square will prepare the damaged trim for repair<sup>18</sup>. Using high-strength marine epoxy ensures that repairs are both invisible and permanent, effectively sealing the new wood against future moisture damage. If your frieze boards are showing signs of wear, it's wise to address the issue promptly to maintain the integrity of your roofline. Ensure your home remains protected from the relentless Seattle rain by inspecting your frieze boards regularly and taking action at the first sign of trouble.
