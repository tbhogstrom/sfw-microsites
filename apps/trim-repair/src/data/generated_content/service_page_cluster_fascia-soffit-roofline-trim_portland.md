## Fascia Board Repair
Fascia boards are critical components of your home's exterior, protecting your roof and walls from moisture damage. In the Pacific Northwest, where rain is frequent, maintaining the integrity of your fascia boards is essential. When fascia boards show signs of rot or damage, it is often tempting to replace the entire board. However, when dealing with valuable or historic woods, such as red-cypress, targeted repairs can be a better option. By cutting back to sound wood and using high-strength epoxy for a butt joint, repairs can be both durable and less invasive than full replacements<sup>1</sup>. This method not only preserves the aesthetic and historical value of your home but also offers a long-lasting solution that can withstand the region's harsh weather conditions<sup>2</sup>. If your fascia boards are showing signs of wear, consider a professional assessment to determine if a repair is the right choice for you.

## Soffit Trim Repair
Soffit trim plays a crucial role in the overall health and aesthetics of your home, especially in the damp climate of Portland, Oregon. Over time, moisture can wreak havoc on soffit materials, leading to rot and deterioration. The key to effective soffit trim repair is addressing these issues promptly to prevent further damage. Start by carefully assessing the extent of the rot. If the damage is minimal, you can replace just the affected section using a precise square cut with a circular saw.<sup>3</sup> For a more durable repair, consider using PVC trim, which resists moisture absorption and reduces the likelihood of future rot.<sup>4</sup> PVC trim can be installed with color-matched nails to blend seamlessly with your existing structure. Proper installation ensures that each component sheds water effectively, keeping your soffit and the underlying structure dry. If you're dealing with soffit trim damage, it's time to take action and protect your home from further moisture intrusion.

## Rotted Fascia Board Replacement
Rotted fascia boards are a common issue for Portland homeowners, particularly due to the persistent rain and moisture typical of the Pacific Northwest climate. When fascia boards become compromised, they can lead to further damage by allowing water to infiltrate the roofline. The most effective way to address rotted fascia is to replace the entire board, especially if the damage is extensive. This involves cutting back to sound wood using a circular saw and speed square for precision<sup>5</sup>. A high-strength marine epoxy can be used to seal joints and ensure lasting durability, even in harsh weather conditions<sup>6</sup>. It's crucial to choose a replacement board that matches the original in species and quality to maintain both the aesthetic and structural integrity of your home. If you're dealing with rotted fascia boards, consider reaching out to SFW Construction to ensure the job is done right, preserving your home against the elements.

## Rafter Tail Trim Repair
Rafter tail trim plays a crucial role in both the aesthetics and protection of your home. In the Pacific Northwest, where rain and moisture are frequent, ensuring these components are in top condition is imperative. Rafter tails, which extend beyond the walls to support the roof eaves, can suffer from rot over time due to water exposure. Repairing these elements involves precisely removing the damaged sections and replacing them with new wood that matches the existing material. A common practice is to make a simple 90-degree cut using a circular saw and a speed square, preparing the damaged area for repair<sup>7</sup>. To ensure a durable and seamless finish, high-strength marine epoxy can be used alongside a plastic biscuit to secure the new section to the existing structure<sup>8</sup>. This method not only restores the integrity of the rafter tails but also extends their lifespan, keeping your home safe from water intrusion. If your rafter tails are showing signs of wear, scheduling a professional inspection and repair will help maintain the structural integrity of your roofline.

## Roofline Trim Repair
Roofline trim is vital for both the aesthetic appeal and structural integrity of your Portland home. Over time, the harsh Pacific Northwest weather can cause damage to roofline trim, compromising its ability to protect your home from moisture ingress. Repairing roofline trim involves assessing the damage and replacing or reinforcing elements like fascia, soffits, and bargeboards. To ensure longevity, it's important to use materials that can withstand our wet climate, such as PVC or treated wood, which are less prone to rot and decay<sup>9</sup>. Proper installation techniques, like using felt paper or peel-and-stick flashing, can protect the underlying structure from water damage<sup>10</sup>. If your roofline trim is showing signs of wear, taking prompt action can prevent more extensive and costly repairs. ## Eave Trim Repair
Eave trim in Portland homes is often exposed to the elements, making it susceptible to damage from rain and moisture common in the Pacific Northwest. Repairing eave trim involves ensuring that all damaged areas are accurately cut back to sound wood, typically using a circular saw for precision<sup>11</sup>. This method prevents further decay by removing all compromised material and providing a clean surface for the new trim piece. Selecting the right materials for repair is crucial; using durable, moisture-resistant wood like cedar, which is factory-primed for added protection, ensures longevity<sup>12</sup>. The repair process is not only about restoring aesthetics but also about preserving the structural integrity of your home's exterior. Once the eave trim is repaired, it is essential to prime and paint it promptly to protect it against future moisture damage. To maintain the beauty and durability of your home’s eaves, consider scheduling a professional inspection and repair service today to address any issues before they escalate.

## Gable Trim Repair
Gable trim repair is essential in maintaining your Portland home’s structural integrity and aesthetic appeal. The gable ends, where the roof meets the siding, are particularly vulnerable to water infiltration due to the Pacific Northwest's persistent rain<sup>13</sup>. Properly fitted trim at these points prevents water damage by sealing gaps that might otherwise allow moisture to seep in. This can lead to rot, mold, and structural damage over time. During a repair, we ensure that all trim is securely fastened and that J-channels are correctly positioned to avoid excessive expansion from heat buildup<sup>14</sup>. If your gable trim is deteriorating, don’t wait for further damage to occur. Reach out to have your gable trim inspected and repaired by our trusted local experts today.

## Barge Board Repair
Barge boards, which run along the gables of a house, are essential for protecting your roof's edges from the Pacific Northwest's notorious rainfall. Over time, moisture can lead to rot and decay, compromising their structural integrity. When addressing a damaged barge board, it's vital to cut back to sound wood using a simple 90º cut with a circular saw and a speed square<sup>15</sup>. This ensures that the new section will bond securely with the existing structure. For a lasting repair, consider using high-strength marine epoxy and a plastic biscuit to join the new and old sections, which helps create an invisible and durable repair<sup>16</sup>. This method withstands Portland's wet climate, reducing the likelihood of future repairs. If your barge boards show signs of damage, ensure they're repaired promptly to maintain the structural integrity and aesthetic appeal of your home. Contact us today to assess and repair your barge boards effectively.

## Frieze Board Repair
Frieze boards play a crucial role in both the aesthetic and functional aspects of your home, concealing the top edge of siding and creating a seamless transition to the eaves<sup>17</sup>. In Portland's damp climate, these boards are particularly susceptible to moisture and rot, making timely repairs essential to prevent further damage. When addressing deteriorated frieze boards, it’s important to cut back to sound wood using a simple 90° cut with a circular saw for a clean edge<sup>18</sup>. This prepares the area for a solid patch or replacement. High-strength marine epoxy is recommended for joining new sections to ensure durability and to withstand the Pacific Northwest's wet conditions<sup>18</sup>. By acting promptly to repair your frieze boards, you maintain the structural integrity and curb appeal of your home. If you notice any signs of rot or damage, reach out to us to schedule a professional assessment and repair service.
