## Corner Board Repair
Corner boards are vital to protecting the edges of your Seattle home from the Pacific Northwest's relentless rain. These boards seal the junctions between siding panels and prevent moisture infiltration. However, given the region's wet climate, corner boards are prone to rot and decay. Repairing them promptly is crucial to maintaining your home's structural integrity. The repair process typically involves cutting back to undamaged wood using a scarf joint, which helps ensure a tight and secure fit<sup>1</sup>. To further improve the durability of the repair, use a high-strength marine epoxy and a plastic biscuit to fasten the new section of trim to the existing board<sup>2</sup>. This method creates a long-lasting bond that resists the seasonal movements that can cause joints to separate. By addressing corner board damage early, you can prevent more extensive repairs down the line. Assess the condition of your corner boards and consider scheduling a consultation to ensure your home remains weather-tight.

## Decorative Trim Repair
Decorative trim can enhance the aesthetic appeal of your Seattle home but can also suffer from wear and tear due to the Pacific Northwest's damp climate. Moisture can seep into wooden trim, leading to swelling, rot, and eventual decay. When repairing decorative trim, it is crucial to meticulously remove the damaged sections without disturbing the surrounding structure. A precise cut with a circular saw ensures that only the compromised wood is extracted<sup>3</sup>. For a seamless repair, consider using high-strength marine epoxy and a plastic biscuit to join the new trim piece to the existing structure<sup>4</sup>. This method not only provides a robust and long-lasting connection but also helps maintain the trim's original appearance. Once the repair is complete, ensure all sides and edges of the trim are primed and painted to protect against future moisture infiltration. If your decorative trim is showing signs of deterioration, act promptly to prevent further damage and preserve the beauty of your home.
