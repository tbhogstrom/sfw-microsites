## Siding & Trim Integration Repair
When it comes to siding and trim integration, precise alignment and sealing are crucial to prevent moisture intrusion, a common issue in Portland's damp climate. Proper integration ensures that the trim and siding form a unified, weather-resistant barrier. One effective method is to leave a small space between the siding and trim, then caulk this gap to prevent water penetration<sup>1</sup>. This technique allows for expansion and contraction while maintaining a tight seal. It's also important to ensure that the weather-resistive barrier, such as housewrap, is properly lapped and taped at these junctions to direct water away from the building envelope<sup>2</sup>. Regular inspections and timely repairs of any gaps or deteriorated caulking can prevent costly water damage. If your siding and trim are not performing as they should, it's time to assess their integration for potential repair needs.

To ensure your siding and trim are effectively integrated, schedule a professional assessment today.

## Trim & Flashing Integration Repair
Integrating trim and flashing is crucial to maintaining the structural integrity of your home, especially in Portland's wet climate. Proper integration ensures that moisture is effectively diverted away from your home, preventing water intrusion and potential damage. Flashing is typically installed to extend past window and door nailing flanges, diverting water away from the building envelope<sup>3</sup>. This helps to protect vulnerable areas where siding meets windows, doors, and other openings. It's essential that flashing overlaps correctly; side flashings should overlap the bottom flashing, and the top flashing should cover the tops of the side flashings<sup>4</sup>. Neglecting this crucial step can lead to water seeping into your home, causing rot and structural damage. If you suspect your home's trim and flashing may not be adequately integrated, it's time to act. Ensure your home remains dry and secure by scheduling a professional evaluation and repair today.

## Trim Water Intrusion Repair
Trim water intrusion can lead to significant damage to your Pacific Northwest home, especially with the persistent rain and moisture typical in the Portland area. When water penetrates exterior trim, it can cause wood rot, mold growth, and structural issues if not promptly addressed. A critical step in repairing water intrusion is to identify the damaged sections of trim and remove them surgically. Using a lightweight trim saw can help to make precise cuts, ensuring that only the damaged wood is removed<sup>5</sup>. Once the compromised trim is removed, replacing it with a new section is essential. Marine-grade epoxy and plastic biscuits provide a strong and durable bond when installing new trim pieces, even in the challenging Portland climate<sup>6</sup>. This method helps prevent future water penetration and extends the life of your home's exterior. If you suspect water intrusion in your trim, don't wait for further damage—schedule a professional assessment today to protect your home.

## Trim & Window Waterproofing Repair
Waterproofing your trim and windows is crucial for preventing moisture damage in the rainy Pacific Northwest climate. The process involves sealing gaps between the window frame and surrounding trim to prevent water infiltration. Self-adhesive flashing tape is often used for this task, as it creates a reliable barrier against moisture. The tape is applied around the perimeter of the window, ensuring a tight seal that prevents water from seeping in<sup>7</sup>. To further enhance protection, a bead of siliconized caulk is applied over the flashing tape, reinforcing the waterproof barrier<sup>8</sup>. Additionally, installing rigid cap flashing above the window diverts water away from the structure, preventing it from pooling and causing leaks. These measures not only protect the structural integrity of your home but also extend the lifespan of your windows and trim. If your Portland home is experiencing water intrusion around windows, it's time to take action and reinforce your waterproofing efforts.

## Trim & Siding Leak Repair
Leaks between trim and siding can lead to significant damage if not addressed promptly. In the Pacific Northwest, where rain is a constant companion, ensuring a tight seal between these components is crucial. When water infiltrates through gaps or improperly sealed areas, it can cause rot, mold, and structural damage over time. To effectively repair leaks, it's essential first to identify the source of the problem. This may involve probing with a pocketknife or awl to detect any rot behind the trim or at siding-trim joints<sup>9</sup>. Once identified, the damaged sections of trim or siding should be carefully removed. It's often possible to repair rather than replace sections of siding with gouges or splits by sealing them in place<sup>10</sup>. To prevent future leaks, ensure all seams are properly sealed with caulk or appropriate flashing techniques. Additionally, priming cut edges and applying a topcoat of acrylic latex paint can help protect against moisture intrusion. Don't let leaks compromise your home—act now to secure your trim and siding against the elements.

## Exterior Trim Waterproofing
In the Pacific Northwest, where rain and moisture are a constant challenge, exterior trim waterproofing is essential for maintaining the integrity and appearance of your home. Proper waterproofing involves ensuring that all trim is sealed to prevent water from seeping in, which can lead to rot and decay over time. The use of high-quality caulking is crucial to seal gaps effectively and allow for the natural expansion and contraction of materials<sup>11</sup>. Additionally, selecting weather-resistant materials like factory-primed finger-jointed cedar can enhance durability<sup>12</sup>. This material is not only stable and takes paint well but also provides a subtle shadowline for an aesthetically pleasing finish. Regular maintenance, including checking for gaps and resealing when necessary, will protect your home from the harsh elements and prolong the life of your exterior trim. If you notice signs of water damage or suspect your trim needs attention, schedule an inspection with our team to ensure your home remains protected.
