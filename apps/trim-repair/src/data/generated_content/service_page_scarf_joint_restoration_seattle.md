## Service Overview

### What is Scarf Joint Restoration?

Scarf joint restoration involves carefully cutting back damaged sections of trim to sound wood, forming a joint at a 45-degree angle (known as a scarf joint), and securely attaching a new piece of trim using high-strength marine epoxy and fasteners. This method not only repairs the damage but also strengthens the connection, ensuring that the joint withstands seasonal movements and moisture exposure [Source: Christina Glennon, Page 196].
