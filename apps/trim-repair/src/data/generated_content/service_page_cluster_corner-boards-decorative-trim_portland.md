## Corner Board Repair
Corner boards are essential components of your home's exterior, protecting the siding and framing from the elements. In Portland's rainy climate, these boards are particularly vulnerable to moisture damage and rot. To repair a damaged corner board, it's crucial to first remove any rotten wood, cutting back to healthy material at a 45-degree angle with precision tools like a circular saw<sup>1</sup>. Use a weather-resistant adhesive, such as a high-strength marine epoxy, to secure a new piece of wood to the existing structure<sup>2</sup>. This ensures a durable bond that can withstand the Pacific Northwest's seasonal changes. After securing the new section, let the adhesive cure fully before sanding and painting the repaired area to match the rest of your home. The final step is to apply a generous bead of caulk along the joints to seal out moisture and prevent future damage. If you're uncertain about tackling this repair yourself, consider reaching out to SFW Construction for expert assistance.

## Decorative Trim Repair
Decorative trim is more than just an aesthetic feature; it plays a crucial role in protecting your home from the elements and concealing construction gaps. In Portland, where the damp climate can take a toll on exterior woodwork, maintaining this trim is essential. Repairs often involve removing the damaged section of trim with a precise cut using a circular saw and Speed Square<sup>3</sup>. This ensures that the patch fits seamlessly and remains durable over time. A high-strength marine epoxy, coupled with a plastic biscuit, can be used to bond the new section to the existing wood, providing a robust and long-lasting repair<sup>4</sup>. Regular upkeep of your decorative trim can prevent moisture from seeping into your home's structure, which can lead to more significant issues down the line. If you notice any signs of damage or rot, it's important to address them promptly to maintain your home's exterior appeal and integrity.
