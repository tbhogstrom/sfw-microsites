## Subfloor Repair
Repairing your subfloor is crucial to maintaining the safety and integrity of your Seattle home, particularly given the Pacific Northwest's notorious rain and moisture. Over time, moisture can seep into the subfloor, leading to rot and potential structural issues. In older homes, subflooring is often made of 1-inch boards laid diagonally, which can shift and create gaps over time<sup>1</sup>. If you notice springy or uneven floors, it could be a sign that your subfloor needs attention. Strengthening the subfloor can involve adding blocking between joists or overlaying with plywood or OSB panels to ensure a flat, solid base<sup>2</sup>. Proper repair not only prevents further damage but also provides a stable surface for any floor refinishing or new flooring installations. If you suspect your subfloor is compromised, it’s time to address the issue before it leads to costlier repairs. Let our team at SFW Construction evaluate and repair your subfloor to keep your home safe and sound.

## Subfloor Replacement
Replacing a subfloor in your Seattle home is a significant but necessary step when dealing with extensive damage, particularly in the wet Pacific Northwest climate. Older homes may have subflooring made from 1-inch boards laid diagonally, which can deteriorate over time, especially with moisture exposure<sup>3</sup>. Modern replacements often use 5/8-inch or thicker performance-rated exterior plywood or oriented strand board (OSB), laid with the face veneer perpendicular to the floor joists for optimal strength and durability<sup>4</sup>. This updated material provides a more resilient barrier against water damage and mold, crucial in Seattle's damp environment. If your subfloor is compromised, it may compromise the entire structural integrity of your floors. The process involves removing the existing subfloor, assessing the condition of the joists, and installing new material, ensuring all edges are properly aligned and gaps are sealed to prevent moisture infiltration. By choosing to replace your subfloor with modern materials, you not only enhance your home's stability but also its value. If you're experiencing subfloor issues, now is the time to consider a professional replacement.

## Subfloor Water Damage Repair
Water damage to subfloors in Seattle homes can quickly escalate into a major issue if not addressed promptly. The Pacific Northwest's damp climate makes it crucial to handle moisture-related problems effectively. Subfloors, typically constructed from materials like CDX plywood or OSB panels, can suffer from rot and mold when exposed to excessive moisture<sup>5</sup>. Homeowners should regularly check moisture levels using a reliable moisture meter to ensure the subfloor's moisture content (MC) remains below 20% to prevent fungal growth<sup>6</sup>. If you find water damage in your subfloor, first identify and fix the source of moisture, which could be from leaking pipes, inadequate roof flashing, or poor drainage systems. Once the source is addressed, replace any damaged sections of the subfloor with new, moisture-resistant materials, ensuring they are properly sealed and ventilated. To prevent future issues, consider installing a vapor barrier and improving crawlspace ventilation. Don't let subfloor water damage compromise your home's structural integrity—take immediate action to protect your investment.

## Sagging Floor System Repair
Sagging floors in Seattle homes often stem from the unique moisture challenges and aging structures typical to the Pacific Northwest. One common cause is excess moisture in crawlspaces, which can lead to the deterioration of wooden posts and floor joists over time<sup>7</sup>. This moisture can cause wood to rot or warp, resulting in uneven or sagging floors. To address this, it's crucial to first eliminate the excess moisture by sealing the crawlspace and ensuring proper drainage and ventilation<sup>8</sup>. After moisture control, the repair process may involve reinforcing existing joists, adding new support beams, or replacing damaged components entirely. These methods ensure that your floor is stable and secure, preventing future sagging and maintaining the structural integrity of your home. ## Floor Joist Replacement
Replacing floor joists in Seattle's older homes often becomes necessary due to moisture issues common in the Pacific Northwest. Floor joists are essential structural components that support the subfloor and, ultimately, the flooring above. Over time, exposure to damp conditions in crawl spaces can lead to wood rot and compromise the integrity of these joists. When replacing floor joists, it's crucial to use materials that resist moisture, such as pressure-treated lumber, to prolong their lifespan<sup>9</sup>. In some cases, installing I-joists can be beneficial due to their strength and stability, as they are less prone to warping and shrinking than traditional lumber<sup>10</sup>. If you're noticing sagging floors or unusual creaking sounds, it may be time to consider floor joist replacement to ensure the safety and stability of your home. ## Floor Joist Reinforcement
Floor joist reinforcement is vital for maintaining the structural integrity of your Seattle home, especially in older buildings that have been exposed to the Pacific Northwest's damp climate. Over time, moisture can weaken wooden joists, leading to sagging floors or even structural failure. Reinforcing floor joists involves adding support to existing structures to handle additional loads and prevent further damage. One effective method is "sistering," which involves attaching a new joist to the old one to double the strength and stability<sup>11</sup>. Alternatively, installing blocking between joists can distribute loads more evenly across the floor system, minimizing movement and springiness<sup>12</sup>. If you're experiencing issues with your floor joists, it's crucial to address them promptly to avoid more extensive repairs down the line. Consult with our experts to determine the best reinforcement strategy for your home.

## Floor System Framing
Framing a floor system is a crucial step in ensuring the structural integrity and longevity of your home, especially in Seattle's damp climate. The floor system typically consists of joists, which are the horizontal structural elements that support the subflooring and finished flooring above. To counteract the effects of moisture and potential wood decay, it's essential to use pressure-treated wood for any components in contact with the ground, such as sill plates and joists<sup>13</sup>. Proper installation requires the alignment of joists perpendicular to a main girder, which runs the length of the house and provides vital support<sup>14</sup>. In older homes, you might encounter balloon framing, but most modern constructions use platform framing due to its efficiency and strength<sup>14</sup>. Ensuring that your floor system is correctly framed can prevent issues like sagging floors and uneven surfaces. If you're unsure about the condition of your floor system or need a professional assessment, consider scheduling a consultation to address any concerns before they escalate into significant structural problems.

## Crawlspace Sagging Floor Repair
Crawlspaces in Seattle homes, often damp and poorly ventilated, can lead to sagging floors when left untreated. Moisture accumulation is a primary cause of wood rot and structural weakness in floor joists and subfloors<sup>15</sup>. To address sagging floors, it's crucial to first manage the moisture by ensuring proper drainage and ventilation. Covering the crawlspace floor with polyethylene can effectively limit moisture and air infiltration<sup>16</sup>. Once moisture issues are controlled, reinforcing or replacing compromised joists and subfloors may be necessary. Sistering new joists to existing ones or adding additional support through blocking can stabilize and level sagging floors. In severe cases, full joist replacement might be required to restore structural integrity. Addressing crawlspace issues not only rectifies sagging floors but also prevents future problems such as mold growth and increased humidity in living spaces. If you notice uneven or soft spots in your floors, it’s time to inspect your crawlspace for damage and take corrective action.
