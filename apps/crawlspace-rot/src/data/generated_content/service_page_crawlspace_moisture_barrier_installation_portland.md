## Service Overview

### What is Crawlspace Moisture Barrier Installation?

Crawlspace moisture barrier installation involves placing a durable, water-resistant barrier over the soil in your crawlspace to prevent moisture from seeping into the area. This process includes the following technical steps:

1. **Assessment of Existing Conditions**: Identifying any pre-existing moisture issues, including standing water and mold growth.
2. **Preparation**: Cleaning the crawlspace to remove debris and ensuring a smooth surface for the barrier installation.
3. **Installation of the Vapor Barrier**: Utilizing high-quality polyethylene sheeting that is at least 6 mil thick, the barrier is laid out to cover the entire floor of the crawlspace, extending up the walls to a height of at least 6 inches. All seams are sealed with compatible tape to create a continuous moisture barrier, as recommended by best practices [Source: Charlie Wing, The Visual Handbook of Building and Remodeling, Page 463].
4. **Securing the Barrier**: The barrier is then secured and sealed at the edges to prevent any moisture from entering, ensuring it is caulked or taped properly [Source: Michael Litchfield, Renovation, Page 414].
